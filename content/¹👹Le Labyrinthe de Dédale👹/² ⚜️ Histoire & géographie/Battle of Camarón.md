The **Battle of Camarón** ([French](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_language "French language"): *Bataille de Camerone*) which occurred over ten hours<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 21">: 21 </span></sup>  on 30 April 1863 between the [Foreign Legion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Foreign_Legion "French Foreign Legion") of the [French Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Army "French Army") and the [Mexican Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexican_Army "Mexican Army"), is regarded as a defining moment in the Foreign Legion's history.

A small infantry patrol, led by Captain [Jean Danjou](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean_Danjou "Jean Danjou") and Lieutenants Clément Maudet and Jean Vilain, numbering just 65 men<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-1" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 5">: 5 </span></sup>  was attacked and besieged by a force that may have eventually reached 3,000 Mexican infantry and cavalry, and was forced to make a defensive stand at the nearby [Hacienda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hacienda "Hacienda") Camarón, in [Camarón de Tejeda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Camar%C3%B3n_de_Tejeda "Camarón de Tejeda"), [Veracruz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Veracruz "Veracruz"), [Mexico](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexico "Mexico").

The conduct of the *Légionnaires* who, overwhelmingly outnumbered, refused to surrender, killing and injuring hundreds of enemy troops before finally succumbing, led to a certain mystique, and the battle of Camarón became synonymous with bravery and a fight-to-the-death attitude.<sup id="cite_ref-Windrow2005_2-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

## Background

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Camar%C3%B3n&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Background")\]

Main article: [Siege of Puebla (1863)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siege_of_Puebla_\(1863\) "Siege of Puebla (1863)")

As part of the [second French intervention in Mexico](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_French_intervention_in_Mexico "Second French intervention in Mexico"), a French army commanded by the [General Forey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%89lie_Fr%C3%A9d%C3%A9ric_Forey "Élie Frédéric Forey") was besieging the Mexican city of [Puebla](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puebla,_Puebla "Puebla, Puebla").<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-2" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: xxii">: xxii </span></sup>  The Legion's 1st and 2nd Battalions, under the command of Col. [Pierre Jeanningros](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pierre_Joseph_Jeanningros "Pierre Joseph Jeanningros"), had arrived in [Veracruz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Veracruz_\(city\) "Veracruz (city)") on 26 March.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-3" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 2–3">: 2–3 </span></sup>  The 2nd Battalion, under the command of Maj. Munier, was assigned to protect the road from Tejeria to Soledad, while the 1st Battalion protected the road onwards to Chiquihuite.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-4" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 4–5">: 4–5 </span></sup> 

In support of the siege, the French sent a convoy, under the command of Capt. Jacques Cabossel, with 3 million francs in gold bullion, siege guns, and sixty wagons of ammunition.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-5" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 11–12">: 11–12 </span></sup>  Two companies of [fusiliers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fusilier "Fusilier") were to escort the convoy from La Soledad to Chiquihuite.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-6" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 7">: 7 </span></sup>  However, Jeanningros sent the 3rd company of the 1st Battalion to reinforce the convoy guard when Indian spies told him the Mexicans would ambush the convoy.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-7" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 7">: 7 </span></sup>  As the company had no officers, Captain Jean Danjou, [adjutant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adjutant "Adjutant") major of the 1st Battalion, volunteered to command.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-8" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 11, 15">: 11, 15 </span></sup>  *Sous-Lieutenants* (2nd Lts.) Napoleon Vilain and Clement Maudet soon volunteered to join him.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-9" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 16–17">: 16–17 </span></sup> 

Six miles northeast of Camarón, was La Joya, headquarters of Col. Francisco de Paula Milán's Center Brigade, consisting of National Guard troops from [Cordoba](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cordoba,_Veracruz "Cordoba, Veracruz"), [Xalapa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xalapa "Xalapa"), [Coscomatepec de Bravo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coscomatepec_de_Bravo "Coscomatepec de Bravo") and Veracruz, plus [dragoons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dragoons "Dragoons") and [lancers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lancers "Lancers").<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-10" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 12–13">: 12–13 </span></sup> 

## The battle

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Battle_of_Camar%C3%B3n&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: The battle")\]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Main_Danjou.gif/250px-Main_Danjou.gif)

[Captain Danjou](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean_Danjou "Jean Danjou")'s prosthetic hand is now the Legion's most venerated [relic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Relic "Relic")

On 30 April, at 01:00, the 3rd company of 62 Legionnaires and three Legion officers were en route from Chiquihuite for Palo Verde, 6 hours and 22 miles away.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-11" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 26">: 26 </span></sup>  At 05:45, they reached Camerone and the La Trinidad Hacienda, its 50 meter long walls forming a square, enclosing a two-story ranch house on the north and an open stable on the south, long ago abandoned.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-12" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 36">: 36 </span></sup> 

They reached Palo Verde as planned by 07:00, and proceeded to prepare their morning coffee.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-13" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 39–40">: 39–40 </span></sup>  Coffee was interrupted by the sighting around 08:00 of Mexican cavalry, Capt. Tomas Algonzanas' [Cotaxtla](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cotaxtla "Cotaxtla") Squadron of 250 *rancheros*, to the north heading to the southeast, but they continued southward, passing the road a half mile away.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-14" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 41–43">: 41–43 </span></sup>  Seeking a more defensible position, Danjou moved his men back west, along the forest north of the road, to the hacienda's ten foot walls.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-15" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 37, 42">: 37, 42 </span></sup>  Milán, meanwhile, decided that he must eliminate Danjou's men before they could discover the size of Milán's forces and their planned ambush of the convoy.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-16" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 48">: 48 </span></sup>  Milán sent his dragoon squadron and four infantry battalions to join Algonzana's lancers.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-17" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 49">: 49 </span></sup>  Danjou's plan was to draw the Mexican forces away from the convoy.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-18" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 48">: 48 </span></sup>  The Legionnaires reached the hacienda by 09:00.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-19" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 50">: 50 </span></sup> 

Danjou decided to proceed west on the road towards Paseo del Macho but had proceeded only a short distance before discovering Algonzana's cavalrymen north of the road, and Danjou ordered his men to form a [hollow square](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infantry_square "Infantry square").<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-20" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 54">: 54 </span></sup>  Charging in two columns, the Mexicans "were stopped short" by salvos and then withdrew, allowing the 3rd to withdraw to the cactus-filled ditch on the south side of the road and make their way back to the hacienda.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-21" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 56–57">: 56–57 </span></sup>  However, on the way, sixteen of Danjou's men were captured, and Danjou faced another Mexican cavalry charge sweeping around the south side of the hacienda.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-22" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 58">: 58 </span></sup>  The second charge was again met by rifle fire from Danjou's square, forcing the Mexicans to ride away.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-23" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 59">: 59 </span></sup>  Upon reaching the hacienda, the Legionnaires were surprised to discover Mexicans occupying the ranch house.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-24" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 61">: 61 </span></sup> 

Milán arrived with the dragoons at about the same time the bullion convoy headed back to La Soledad, after being warned of the 3rd's difficulties.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-25" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 66">: 66 </span></sup>  Seeing that he was totally surrounded, Capt. Danjou "urged his men to take an oath to fight to the death rather than surrender... he made them swear their fealty on his wooden hand.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-26" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 69">: 69 </span></sup>  Danjou then shared his bottle of wine and encouraged his men with "those noble words that warm one's heart and makes the final sacrifice less difficult to face."<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-27" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 70">: 70 </span></sup> 

Milán sent Capt. Ramon Laine to negotiate a surrender,<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-28" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 67">: 67 </span></sup>  who explained to Danjou, "you will be needlessly slaughtered."<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-29" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 70">: 70 </span></sup>  Danjou replied that he "had plenty of ammunition and shall continue to fight."<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-30" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 70">: 70 </span></sup>  At about 11:00, the Mexicans charged the gateways on the west end of the hacienda, and the breach on the south.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-31" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 71">: 71 </span></sup>  Though the attack was held off, Danjou was hit in the chest and soon died.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-32" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 75">: 75 </span></sup>  [Lieutenant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Foreign_Legion "French Foreign Legion") Vilain assumed command.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-33" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 76">: 76 </span></sup>  Casualties mounted on both sides, but amongst the Legionnaires, "water and food, the two most required necessities, were not to be found. Thirst, heat exhaustion, and sunstroke had replaced them."<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-34" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 77">: 77 </span></sup>  Their mules had run off after the first encounter with the Mexican cavalry, and then Col. Milán's Center Brigade infantry, 1200-1400 men, arrived at the hacienda.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-35" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 56, 78">: 56, 78 </span></sup>  When Capt. Laine offered the legionnaires a second chance to surrender, [Sergeant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Foreign_Legion "French Foreign Legion") Vincent Morzycki responded in the spirit of [Pierre Cambronne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pierre_Cambronne "Pierre Cambronne"), with "*Merde*."<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-36" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 79">: 79 </span></sup>  After four hours of fighting, thirty-two Legionnaires remained fighting.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-37" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 81">: 81 </span></sup>  By 14:00, only twenty could still fight.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-38" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 83">: 83 </span></sup>  At 14:30, Lt. Vilain was struck dead and [Lieutenant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Foreign_Legion "French Foreign Legion") Maudet took command.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-39" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 84">: 84 </span></sup> 

By 17:00, the roof of the ranch house had been burned away, the Legionnaires were down to twelve men, and Col. Milan ordered his men out of the hacienda, and offered the Legionnaires a third chance to surrender.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-40" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 85–86">: 85–86 </span></sup>  In the next onslaught, [Corporals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Foreign_Legion "French Foreign Legion") Everiste Burg, Karl Magnin and Heinrich Pinzinger, plus fusiliers Leon Gorski and Hippolyte Kunnasseg were captured.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-41" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 86–87">: 86–87 </span></sup>  By 17:30, only Lt. Maudet's men remained in the stable.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-42" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 87">: 87 </span></sup> 

By 18:00, with ammunition exhausted, the last of Danjou's men, numbering only five, including Lt. Maudet, desperately mounted a [bayonet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bayonet "Bayonet") charge.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-43" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 90">: 90 </span></sup>  Two men fell outright, and the rest were surrounded.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-44" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 91">: 91 </span></sup>  One of them, Victor Catteau, had leapt in front of Maudet in an effort to protect him, and died in the Mexican barrage.<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-45" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 91">: 91 </span></sup>  Major Campos ordered the Legionnaires to surrender to which [Corporal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Foreign_Legion "French Foreign Legion") Phillipe Maine answered, "We will surrender if you leave us our weapons and our equipment. You also have to promise to take care of our wounded lieutenant."<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-46" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 91">: 91 </span></sup> 

When Campos brought the trio to Milán, he asked, "Is this all of them? Is this all of the men who are left?" Then, in amazement, he exclaimed, "These are not men! They are demons!"<sup id="cite_ref-Ryan_1-47" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 92–93">: 92–93 </span></sup> 
## Chronologie de la Bataille de Camerone

> [!timeline|t-l] **Fin avril 1863** 
> Le régiment étranger reçoit l'ordre d'escorter un convoi d'or, d'argent et de munitions vers Puebla.

> [!timeline|t-r] **Fin avril 1863** 
> Le capitaine d'Ajot propose une mission de reconnaissance avec 64 hommes de la troisième compagnie.

> [!timeline|t-l] **30 avril 1863** _matin_ 
> Après une marche forcée, la troupe d'Ajot arrive près de Camerone.

> [!timeline|t-r] **30 avril 1863** _8h_ 
> Ils sont surpris par un tir ennemi et se retranchent dans l'hacienda de Camerone.

> [!timeline|t-l] **30 avril 1863** _9h_ 
> Défense organisée dans l'hacienda. Le capitaine d'Ajot fait prêter serment à ses hommes de ne jamais se rendre.

> [!timeline|t-r] **30 avril 1863** _11h_ 
> L'ennemi encercle complètement leur position.

> [!timeline|t-l] **30 avril 1863** _14h_ 
> Le sous-lieutenant Jeanvilain est mis hors de combat ou tué.

> [!timeline|t-r] **30 avril 1863** _Journée_ 
> Le capitaine d'Ajot et le sous-lieutenant Clément Modet sont mis hors de combat. Le commandement revient au caporal Philippe Men.

> [!timeline|t-l] **30 avril 1863** _17h_ 
> À court de munitions, le caporal Men refuse de se rendre sans garanties du colonel mexicain Milan.

> [!timeline|t-r] **30 avril 1863** _Fin de journée_ 
> Le colonel Milan, respectueux de leur sacrifice, accepte les conditions de Men.

> [!timeline|t-l t-1] **3 mai 1863** _Trois jours après_ 
> Le convoi que les légionnaires devaient protéger part de Veracruz et arrive sans problème à Puebla.
