La **guerre de Crimée** <sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket"><font style="vertical-align: inherit;"><font style="vertical-align: inherit;">[</font></font></span><font style="vertical-align: inherit;"><font style="vertical-align: inherit;"> d </font></font><span class="cite-bracket"><font style="vertical-align: inherit;"><font style="vertical-align: inherit;">]</font></font></span></a></sup> a été menée entre l' [Empire russe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_Empire "Empire russe") et une alliance de l' [Empire ottoman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Empire "Empire ottoman") , du [Second Empire français](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_French_Empire "Second Empire français") , du [Royaume-Uni de Grande-Bretagne et d'Irlande](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland "Royaume-Uni de Grande-Bretagne et d'Irlande") et du [Royaume de Sardaigne-Piémont](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Sardinia_\(1720%E2%80%931861\) "Royaume de Sardaigne (1720–1861)") d'octobre 1853 à février 1856. <sup id="cite_ref-Britannica_13-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-Britannica-13"><span class="cite-bracket"><font style="vertical-align: inherit;"><font style="vertical-align: inherit;">[</font></font></span><font style="vertical-align: inherit;"><font style="vertical-align: inherit;"> 9 </font></font><span class="cite-bracket"><font style="vertical-align: inherit;"><font style="vertical-align: inherit;">]</font></font></span></a></sup> Les causes géopolitiques de la guerre comprenaient la « [question orientale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_question "question orientale") » ( [le déclin de l'Empire ottoman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Decline_and_modernization_of_the_Ottoman_Empire "Déclin et modernisation de l'Empire ottoman") , « l' [homme malade de l'Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sick_man_of_Europe "L'homme malade de l'Europe") »), l'expansion de la Russie impériale dans les [guerres russo-turques](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russo-Turkish_wars "guerres russo-turques") précédentes et la préférence britannique et française de préserver l'Empire ottoman pour maintenir l' [équilibre des pouvoirs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_balance_of_power "équilibre des pouvoirs européens") dans le [Concert de l'Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Concert_of_Europe "Concert de l'Europe") .

Le point d'éclair fut un conflit entre la France et la Russie au sujet des droits des minorités [catholiques](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic_Church "Église catholique") et [orthodoxes en](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Orthodox_Church "Église orthodoxe orientale") [Palestine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palestine_\(region\) "Palestine (région)") . <sup id="cite_ref-GG-NGG-CAS-C1843-MP-CNMP-X91_14-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-GG-NGG-CAS-C1843-MP-CNMP-X91-14"><span class="cite-bracket"><font style="vertical-align: inherit;"><font style="vertical-align: inherit;">[</font></font></span><font style="vertical-align: inherit;"><font style="vertical-align: inherit;"> 10 </font></font><span class="cite-bracket"><font style="vertical-align: inherit;"><font style="vertical-align: inherit;">]</font></font></span></a></sup> Après que la [Sublime Porte](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sublime_Porte "Sublime Porte") eut refusé la demande [du tsar Nicolas Ier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicholas_I_of_Russia "Nicolas Ier de Russie") de placer les sujets orthodoxes de l'Empire sous sa protection, les troupes russes occupèrent les [principautés danubiennes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Danubian_Principalities "Principautés danubiennes") en juillet 1853. Les Ottomans déclarèrent la guerre à la Russie en octobre <sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket"><font style="vertical-align: inherit;"><font style="vertical-align: inherit;">[</font></font></span><font style="vertical-align: inherit;"><font style="vertical-align: inherit;"> 11 </font></font><span class="cite-bracket"><font style="vertical-align: inherit;"><font style="vertical-align: inherit;">]</font></font></span></a></sup> et stoppèrent l'avancée russe à [Silistrie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siege_of_Silistria_\(1854\) "Siège de Silistrie (1854)") . Craignant la croissance de l'influence russe et poussées par l'indignation publique suscitée par l'anéantissement de l'escadre ottomane à [Sinop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Sinop "Bataille de Sinop") , la Grande-Bretagne et la France rejoignirent la guerre aux côtés des Ottomans en mars 1854. <sup id="cite_ref-Britannica_13-1" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-Britannica-13"><span class="cite-bracket"><font style="vertical-align: inherit;"><font style="vertical-align: inherit;">[</font></font></span><font style="vertical-align: inherit;"><font style="vertical-align: inherit;"> 9 </font></font><span class="cite-bracket"><font style="vertical-align: inherit;"><font style="vertical-align: inherit;">]</font></font></span></a></sup>

En septembre 1854, après de longs préparatifs, les forces alliées débarquèrent en [Crimée](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crimea "Crimée") pour tenter de s'emparer de [Sébastopol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sevastopol "Sevastopol") , principale base navale russe en [mer Noire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Sea "mer Noire") . Elles remportèrent une victoire rapide à la [bataille de l'Alma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_the_Alma "Battle of the Alma") . Les Russes contre-attaquèrent fin octobre lors de ce qui devint la [bataille de Balaclava](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Balaclava "Battle of Balaclava") et furent repoussés. Une seconde contre-attaque à [Inkerman se solda par une impasse. Le front s'engagea dans le](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Inkerman "Battle of Inkerman") [siège de Sébastopol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siege_of_Sevastopol_\(1854%E2%80%931855\) "Siege of Sevastopol (1854–1855)") , qui dura onze mois , entraînant des conditions de combat difficiles pour les troupes des deux camps. Des actions militaires de moindre envergure eurent lieu dans le [Caucase](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caucasus "Caucasus") (1853-1855), en [mer Blanche](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/White_Sea "White Sea") (juillet-août 1854) et dans le [Pacifique Nord](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Pacific "North Pacific") (1854-1855). Le royaume de Sardaigne-Piémont entra aux côtés des alliés en 1855. "Siège de Sébastopol (1854-1855)")Sébastopol finit par tomber après un nouvel [assaut français sur le réduit de Malakoff](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Malakoff "Bataille de Malakoff") en septembre 1855. Isolée et confrontée à la perspective d'une invasion occidentale si la guerre se poursuivait, la Russie [demanda la paix](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suing_for_peace "Demander la paix") en mars 1856. Face à l'impopularité intérieure du conflit, la France et la Grande-Bretagne saluèrent cette décision. Le [traité de Paris](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_\(1856\) "Traité de Paris (1856)") , signé le 30 mars 1856, mit fin à la guerre. Il interdisait à la Russie de baser des navires de guerre en mer Noire. Les [États vassaux](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vassal_state "État vassal") ottomans de [Valachie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wallachia "Valachie") et [de Moldavie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moldavia "Moldavie") devinrent largement indépendants. Les chrétiens de l'Empire ottoman obtinrent une certaine égalité officielle, et l'Église orthodoxe reprit le contrôle des églises chrétiennes en litige. <sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010415_16-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010415-16"><span class="cite-bracket"><font style="vertical-align: inherit;"><font style="vertical-align: inherit;">[</font></font></span><font style="vertical-align: inherit;"><font style="vertical-align: inherit;"> 12 </font></font><span class="cite-bracket"><font style="vertical-align: inherit;"><font style="vertical-align: inherit;">]</font></font></span></a></sup>

The Crimean War was one of the first conflicts in which military forces used modern technologies such as explosive naval [shells](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shell_\(projectile\) "Coquille (projectile)"), [railways](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Railways "Chemins de fer") and [telegraphs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Telegraphy "Télégraphie").<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERoyle2000Preface_17-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTERoyle2000Preface-17"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> It was also one of the first to be documented extensively in written reports and in [photographs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/War_photography "Photographie de guerre"). The war quickly symbolized logistical, medical and tactical failures and mismanagement. The reaction in Britain led to a demand for the [professionalization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Professionalization "Professionnalisation") of medicine, most famously achieved by [Florence Nightingale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Florence_Nightingale "Florence Nightingale"), who gained worldwide attention for pioneering modern [nursing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nursing "Allaitement") while she treated the wounded.

The Crimean War also marked a turning point for the Russian Empire. It weakened the [Imperial Russian Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imperial_Russian_Army "Armée impériale russe"), drained the treasury and undermined its influence in Europe. The humiliating defeat forced Russia's educated elites to identify the country's fundamental problems. It became a catalyst for [reforms of Russia's social institutions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government_reforms_of_Alexander_II_of_Russia "Les réformes gouvernementales d'Alexandre II de Russie"), including the [emancipation reform of 1861](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emancipation_reform_of_1861 "Réforme d'émancipation de 1861") which abolished [serfdom in Russia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serfdom_in_Russia "Le servage en Russie"), and overhauls in the justice system, local self-government, education and military service.

## Eastern question

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Crimean_War&action=edit&section=1 "Modifier la section : Question orientale")\]

See also: [Eastern question](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_question "question orientale")

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Southeast_Europe_1812_map_en.PNG/250px-Southeast_Europe_1812_map_en.PNG)

Southeastern Europe after the [Treaty of Bucharest (1812)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Bucharest_\(1812\) "Traité de Bucarest (1812)")

As the Ottoman Empire [steadily weakened](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Decline_and_modernization_of_the_Ottoman_Empire "Déclin et modernisation de l'Empire ottoman") during the 19th century, the Russian Empire stood poised to take advantage by expanding southward. In the 1850s, the British and the French Empires were allied with the Ottoman Empire and were determined to prevent that from happening.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The historian [A. J. P. Taylor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A._J._P._Taylor "AJP Taylor") argued that the war had resulted not from aggression, but from the interacting fears of the major players:

> In some sense the Crimean War was predestined and had deep-seated causes. Neither Nicholas I nor Napoleon III nor the British government could retreat from the conflict for prestige once it was launched. Nicholas needed a subservient Turkey for the sake of Russian security; Napoleon needed success for the sake of his domestic position; the British government needed an independent Turkey for the security of the Eastern Mediterranean... Mutual fear, not mutual aggression, caused the Crimean War.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETaylor195460–61_19-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTETaylor195460%E2%80%9361-19"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

### Weakening of the Ottoman Empire: 1820–1840s

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Crimean_War&action=edit&section=2 "Modifier la section : Affaiblissement de l'Empire ottoman : années 1820-1840")\]

In the early 1800s, the Ottoman Empire suffered a number of existential challenges. The [Serbian Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serbian_Revolution "Révolution serbe") in 1804 resulted in the autonomy of the first [Balkan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balkan "balkanique") Christian nation under the empire. The [Greek War of Independence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_War_of_Independence "Guerre d'indépendance grecque"), which began in early 1821, provided further evidence of the empire's internal and military weakness, and the commission of atrocities by Ottoman military forces (see [Chios massacre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chios_massacre "massacre de Chios")) further undermined the empire. The disbandment of the centuries-old [Janissary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Janissary "Janissaire") corps by Sultan [Mahmud II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mahmud_II "Mahmoud II") on 15 June 1826 ([Auspicious Incident](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Auspicious_Incident "Incident propice")) helped the empire in the longer term but deprived it of its existing standing army in the short term.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipédia&nbsp;: Veuillez clarifier"><span title="Le texte à proximité de cette balise pourrait nécessiter une clarification ou la suppression de jargon. (février 2017)">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In 1827, the Anglo-Franco-Russian fleet destroyed almost all of the Ottoman naval forces at the [Battle of Navarino](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Navarino "Bataille de Navarin"). In 1830, [Greece](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Greece "Royaume de Grèce") became independent after ten years of war and the [Russo-Turkish War (1828–29)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russo-Turkish_War_\(1828%E2%80%9329\) "Guerre russo-turque (1828-1829)"). The [Treaty of Adrianople (1829)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Adrianople_\(1829\) "Traité d'Andrinople (1829)") granted Russian and Western European commercial ships free passage through the [Black Sea straits](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dardanelles "Dardanelles"). Also, the Danubian Principalities ([Moldavia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moldavia "Moldavie") and [Wallachia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wallachia "Valachie")) became territories under Russian protection.

France took the opportunity to [occupy Algeria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_conquest_of_Algeria "Conquête française de l'Algérie"), which had been under Ottoman rule, in 1830. In 1831, [Muhammad Ali of Egypt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad_Ali_of_Egypt "Muhammad Ali of Egypt"), the most powerful [vassal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vassal "Vassal") of the Ottoman Empire, declared independence. Ottoman forces were [defeated in a number of battles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egyptian%E2%80%93Ottoman_War_\(1831%E2%80%931833\) "Egyptian–Ottoman War (1831–1833)"), which forced Mahmud II to seek Russian military aid. A Russian army of 10,000 landed on the shores of the [Bosphorus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bosphorus "Bosphorus") in 1833 and helped prevent the Egyptians from capturing [Constantinople](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constantinople "Constantinople").

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e8/Navarino.jpg/250px-Navarino.jpg)

The naval [Battle of Navarino](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Navarino "Battle of Navarino") (1827), as depicted by [Ambroise Louis Garneray](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ambroise_Louis_Garneray "Ambroise Louis Garneray").

"The reasons for the Tsar's disquietude are not obscure. Not Turkey alone was threatened by the advance of [Ibrahim](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ibrahim_Pasha_of_Egypt "Ibrahim Pasha of Egypt"). The rights secured to Russia by a succession of treaties were also directly jeopardized. The substitution of a virile Albanian dynasty at Constantinople in place of the effete [Osmanlis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_dynasty "Ottoman dynasty") was the last thing desired by the Power which wished, naturally enough, to command the gate into the Mediterranean".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMarriott1917222_20-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMarriott1917222-20"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Russia was satisfied with the weak government in Constantinople (Istanbul).

As a result, the [Treaty of Hünkâr İskelesi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_H%C3%BCnk%C3%A2r_%C4%B0skelesi "Treaty of Hünkâr İskelesi") was signed and greatly benefited Russia. It provided for a military alliance between the Russian and the Ottoman Empires if one of them was attacked, and a secret additional clause allowed the Ottomans to opt out of sending troops but to close the Straits to foreign warships if Russia were under threat. [Egypt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egypt_Eyalet "Egypt Eyalet") remained nominally under Ottoman sovereignty but was *de facto* independent.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>

In 1838 in a situation similar to that of 1831, Muhammad Ali of Egypt was not happy about his lack of control and power in [Syria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Syria "Ottoman Syria"), and he [resumed military action](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egyptian%E2%80%93Ottoman_War_\(1839%E2%80%931841\) "Egyptian–Ottoman War (1839–1841)"). The Ottomans lost to the Egyptians at the [Battle of Nezib](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Nezib "Battle of Nezib") on 24 June 1839 but were saved by Britain, [Austria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austrian_Empire "Austrian Empire"), [Prussia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prussia "Prussia") and Russia, who signed a [convention in London](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Convention_of_London_\(1840\) "Convention of London (1840)") on 15 July 1840 that granted Muhammad Ali and his descendants the right to inherit power in Egypt in exchange for the removal of Egyptian forces from Syria and [Lebanon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lebanon "Lebanon"). Moreover, Muhammad Ali had to admit a formal dependence on the Ottoman sultan. After Muhammad Ali refused to obey the requirements of the convention, the allied Anglo-Austrian fleet blockaded the [Nile Delta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nile_Delta "Nile Delta"), bombarded [Beirut](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beirut "Beirut") and [captured Acre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Acre_\(1840\) "Battle of Acre (1840)"). Muhammad Ali then accepted the convention's conditions.

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Interview_with_Mehemet_Ali_in_his_Palace_at_Alexandria%2C_by_David_Roberts_and_Louis_Hague.jpg/250px-Interview_with_Mehemet_Ali_in_his_Palace_at_Alexandria%2C_by_David_Roberts_and_Louis_Hague.jpg)

[Muhammad Ali of Egypt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad_Ali_of_Egypt "Muhammad Ali of Egypt") deposed the Ottoman governor and seized control of Egypt for himself

On 13 July 1841, after the expiry of the Treaty of Hünkâr İskelesi, the [London Straits Convention](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/London_Straits_Convention "London Straits Convention") was signed under pressure from the European countries. The new treaty deprived Russia of its right to block warships from passing into the Black Sea in case of war. Thus, the way to the Black Sea was open for British and French warships during a possible Russo-Ottoman conflict.

Russian historians tend to view that history as evidence that Russia lacked aggressive plans. The Russian historian V. N. Vinogradov writes: "The signing of the documents was the result of deliberate decisions: instead of bilateral (none of the great powers recognized this Treaty of Unkiar Skelessi), the new Treaty of London was obligatory for all, it closed the Bosphorus and Dardanelles. In the absence of expansion plans, this was a sound decision".<sup id="cite_ref-Vinogradov_21-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-Vinogradov-21"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material near this tag needs to be fact-checked with the cited source(s). (August 2020)">verification needed</span></a></i>]</sup>

In 1838, Britain lost interest in crushing the Ottoman Empire. On the contrary, after the conclusion of the trade treaty of 1838 (see [Treaty of Balta Liman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Balta_Liman "Treaty of Balta Liman")), Britain received unlimited access to the markets of the Ottoman Empire. "Britain imposed on the Porte a Tariff Convention which in effect transformed the Ottoman Empire into a virtual free-trade zone.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges201132_22-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges201132-22"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Therefore its trade interests pushed it to protect the integrity of the Ottoman Empire. In the long term, the Ottoman Empire lost the opportunity to modernize and industrialize, but in the short term, it gained the opportunity to receive the support of European powers (primarily Britain) in opposing the desire of the conquered peoples for self-determination and Russia, which sought to crush its influence in the [Balkans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balkans "Balkans") and Asia.

Publicly, European politicians made broad promises to the Ottomans. Lord [Palmerston](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henry_John_Temple,_3rd_Viscount_Palmerston "Henry John Temple, 3rd Viscount Palmerston"), the British [Foreign Secretary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foreign_Secretary_\(United_Kingdom\) "Foreign Secretary (United Kingdom)"), said in 1839: "All that we hear about the decay of the Turkish Empire, and its being a dead body or a sapless trunk, and so forth, is pure and unadulterated nonsense. Given 10 years of peace under European protection, coupled with internal reform, there seemed to him no reason why it should not become again a respectable Power".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMarriott1917214_23-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMarriott1917214-23"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

[Orlando Figes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orlando_Figes "Orlando Figes") has claimed that "The motives of the British in promoting liberal reforms were not just to secure the independence of the Ottoman Empire against Russia. They were also to promote the influence of Britain in Turkey", also: "to promote British free-trade interests (which may have sounded splendid but was arguably damaging to the Ottoman Empire)".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges201158_24-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges201158-24"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

"British exports to the Ottoman Empire, including Egypt and the Danubian principalities, increased nearly threefold from 1840 to 1851 (...) Thus it was very important, from the financial point of view, for Britain to prevent the Ottoman Empire from falling into other hands."<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBadem201059_25-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBadem201059-25"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

"From this moment (1838) the export of British manufactured goods to Turkey rose steeply. There was an elevenfold increase by 1850".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges201132_22-1" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges201132-22"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

Assistance from Western European powers or Russia had twice saved the Ottoman Empire from destruction, but the Ottomans also lost their independence in foreign policy. Britain and France desired more than any other states to preserve the integrity of the Ottoman Empire because they did not want to see Russia gaining access to the [Mediterranean Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea "Mediterranean Sea"). Austria had the same fears.

### Russian expansionism

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Crimean_War&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Russian expansionism")\]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/Siege_of_Varna_1828.jpg/250px-Siege_of_Varna_1828.jpg)

Russian [siege of Varna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siege_of_Varna_\(1828\) "Siege of Varna (1828)") in [Ottoman-ruled Bulgaria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Bulgaria "Ottoman Bulgaria"), July–September 1828

Russia, as a member of the [Holy Alliance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Holy_Alliance "Holy Alliance"), had operated as the "police of Europe" to maintain the [balance of power](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balance_of_power_\(international_relations\) "Balance of power (international relations)") that had been established in the [Congress of Vienna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Congress_of_Vienna "Congress of Vienna") in 1815. Russia had assisted Austria's efforts in suppressing the [Hungarian Revolution of 1848](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hungarian_Revolution_of_1848 "Hungarian Revolution of 1848"), and expected a free hand in settling its problems with the Ottoman Empire, the "[sick man of Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sick_man_of_Europe "Sick man of Europe")". However, Britain could not tolerate Russian dominance of Ottoman affairs, which would challenge its domination of the eastern Mediterranean.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

Starting with [Peter the Great](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_the_Great "Peter the Great") in the early 1700s, after centuries of Ottoman [northward expansion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_wars_in_Europe "Ottoman wars in Europe") and [Crimean-Nogai raids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crimean%E2%80%93Nogai_slave_raids_in_Eastern_Europe "Crimean–Nogai slave raids in Eastern Europe"), Russia began a [southwards expansion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russo-Turkish_War_\(1686%E2%80%931700\) "Russo-Turkish War (1686–1700)") across the sparsely-populated "[Wild Fields](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wild_Fields "Wild Fields")" toward the warm water ports of the Black Sea, which does not freeze over, unlike the handful of ports controlled by Russia in the north. The goal was to promote year-round trade and a year-round navy.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges201011_27-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges201011-27"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Pursuit of that goal brought the emerging Russian state into conflict with the [Ukrainian Cossacks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ukrainian_Cossacks "Ukrainian Cossacks") and then the [Tatars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crimean_Tatars "Crimean Tatars") of the [Crimean Khanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crimean_Khanate "Crimean Khanate")<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> and [Circassians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circassians "Circassians").<sup id="cite_ref-James_Stanislaus_Bell_29-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-James_Stanislaus_Bell-29"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

"The plan to develop Russia as a southern power had begun in earnest in 1776, when [Catherine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catherine_the_Great "Catherine the Great") placed [Potemkin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grigory_Potemkin "Grigory Potemkin") in charge of New Russia ([Novorossiya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Novorossiya "Novorossiya")), the sparsely populated territories newly conquered from the Ottomans on the Black Sea's northern coastline, and ordered him to colonize the area".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges201123_30-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges201123-30"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> When Russia conquered those groups and gained possession of their territories, the Ottoman Empire lost its buffer zone against Russian expansion, and both empires came into direct conflict. The conflict with the Ottoman Empire also presented a religious issue of importance, as Russia saw itself as the protector of history of the Eastern Orthodox Church under the [Ottoman Orthodox Christians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christianity_in_the_Ottoman_Empire "Christianity in the Ottoman Empire"), who were [legally treated as second-class citizens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dhimmi "Dhimmi").<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010ch._1_31-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010ch._1-31"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The [Ottoman Reform Edict of 1856](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Reform_Edict_of_1856 "Ottoman Reform Edict of 1856"), promulgated after the war, largely reversed much of the second-class status, most notably the [tax that only non-Muslims paid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jizya "Jizya").<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>28<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

Britain's immediate fear was Russia's expansion at the expense of the Ottoman Empire. The British desired to preserve Ottoman integrity and were concerned that Russia might make advances toward [British India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_India "British India") or move toward [Scandinavia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scandinavia "Scandinavia") or [Western Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Europe "Western Europe"). A distraction (in the form of the Ottoman Empire) on the Russian southwest flank would mitigate that threat. The [Royal Navy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Royal_Navy "Royal Navy") also wanted to forestall the threat of a powerful [Imperial Russian Navy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imperial_Russian_Navy "Imperial Russian Navy").<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>29<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation cites a broad range of pages. Page ranges should be limited to one or two pages when possible. (January 2022)"><span class="nowrap">page range too broad</span></span></a></i>]</sup> Taylor stated the British perspective:

> The Crimean war was fought for the sake of Europe rather than for the Eastern question; it was fought against Russia, not in favour of Turkey.... The British fought Russia out of resentment and supposed that her defeat would strengthen the European Balance of Power.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETaylor195461_34-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTETaylor195461-34"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>30<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Kars_1828.jpg/250px-Kars_1828.jpg)

Russian siege of [Kars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kars "Kars"), [Russo-Turkish War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russo-Turkish_War_\(1828%E2%80%9329\) "Russo-Turkish War (1828–29)") of 1828–1829

Because of "British commercial and strategic interests in the Middle East and India",<sup id="cite_ref-RCowley_35-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-RCowley-35"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> the British joined the French, "cement\[ing\] an alliance with Britain and... reassert\[ing\] its military power".<sup id="cite_ref-RCowley_35-1" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-RCowley-35"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Among those who supported the British strategy were [Karl Marx](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karl_Marx "Karl Marx") and [Friedrich Engels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Friedrich_Engels "Friedrich Engels").<sup id="cite_ref-:1_36-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/:1-36"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> In his articles for the *[New-York Tribune](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New-York_Tribune "New-York Tribune")* around 1853, Marx saw the Crimean War as a conflict between the democratic ideals of the west that started with the "great movement of 1789" against "Russia and Absolutism". He described the Ottoman Empire as a buffer against a pattern of expansionism by the Tsar.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-37"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Marx and Engels also accused Lord Palmerston of playing along with the interests of Russia and being unserious in preparing for the conflict.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:02_39-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/:02-39"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>35<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_36-1" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/:1-36"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Marx believed Palmerston to be bribed by Russia, and shared this belief with [David Urquhart](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Urquhart "David Urquhart").<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>36<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:02_39-1" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/:02-39"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>35<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Urquhart, for his part, was a British politician who was a major advocate for the Ottoman Empire.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

[Mikhail Pogodin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mikhail_Pogodin "Mikhail Pogodin"), a professor of history at [Moscow University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moscow_State_University "Moscow State University"), gave Nicholas I a summary of Russia's policy towards the Slavs in the war. Nicholas' answer was filled with grievances against the West. Nicholas shared Pogodin's sense that Russia's role as the protector of [Orthodox Christians in the Ottoman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christianity_in_the_Ottoman_Empire "Christianity in the Ottoman Empire") was not understood and that Russia was unfairly treated by the West. Nicholas especially approved of the following passage:<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2011134_43-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2011134-43"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>39<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

> [France takes Algeria from Turkey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_conquest_of_Algeria "French conquest of Algeria"), and almost every year [England annexes another Indian principality](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Company_rule_in_India "Company rule in India"): none of this disturbs the balance of power; but when Russia occupies [Moldavia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moldavia "Moldavia") and [Wallachia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wallachia "Wallachia"), albeit only temporarily, that disturbs the balance of power. [France occupies Rome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Republic_\(19th_century\)#French_siege "Roman Republic (19th century)") and stays there several years during peacetime: that is nothing; but Russia only thinks of occupying Constantinople, and the peace of Europe is threatened. [The English declare war on the Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Opium_War "First Opium War"), who have, it seems, offended them: no one has the right to intervene; but Russia is obliged to ask Europe for permission if it quarrels with its neighbour. England threatens [Greece](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Greece "Kingdom of Greece") to support the [false claims](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Don_Pacifico_affair "Don Pacifico affair") of a miserable Jew and burns its fleet: that is a lawful action; but Russia demands a treaty to protect millions of Christians, and that is deemed to strengthen its position in the East at the expense of the balance of power. We can expect nothing from the West but blind hatred and malice.... (*comment in the margin by Nicholas I*: 'This is the whole point').
> 
> — Mikhail Pogodin's memorandum to Nicholas I, 1853<sup id="cite_ref-slate_44-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-slate-44"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>40<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

Russia was militarily weak, technologically backward and administratively incompetent. Despite its grand ambitions toward the south, it had not built its railway network in that direction, and its communications were poor. Its bureaucracy was riddled with graft, corruption and inefficiency and was unprepared for war. Its navy was weak and technologically backward. Its army, although very large, suffered from colonels who pocketed their men's pay, from poor morale, and from a technological deficit relative to Britain and France. By the war's end, the profound weaknesses of the Russian armed forces had become readily apparent, and the Russian leadership was determined to reform it.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>41<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>42<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

However, no matter how great the problems of Russia were, Russia believed those of the Ottomans were greater. "In a one-to-one fight Nikolai (Tsar) had no doubt of beating the Ottoman armies and navy".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBadem201062_47-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBadem201062-47"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>43<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Russian foreign policy failed to understand the importance of Britain's trade interests and did not understand the changes in the situation after the conclusion of the Anglo-Ottoman Treaty in 1838 (see [Treaty of Balta Liman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Balta_Liman "Treaty of Balta Liman")). Russia attempted to "honestly" negotiate with the United Kingdom on the partition of the Ottoman Empire and made concessions in order to eliminate all objections from the United Kingdom.

"The Tsar Nicholas had always, as we have seen, been anxious to maintain a cordial understanding with England in regard to the Eastern Question, and early in the spring of 1853 he had a series of interviews with Sir [George Hamilton Seymour](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Hamilton_Seymour "George Hamilton Seymour"), then [British ambassador](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Ambassador_to_Russia "British Ambassador to Russia") at St. Petersburg."<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMarriott1917229_48-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMarriott1917229-48"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>44<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Emperor Nicholas I assured that he did not intend to seize Constantinople and territories in the Balkans, he himself offered Britain to take over Egypt and Crete.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMarriott1917230_49-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMarriott1917230-49"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>45<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Concessions at the conclusion of the [London Straits Convention](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/London_Straits_Convention "London Straits Convention") were made earlier in 1841. "By signing the convention, the Russians had given up their privileged position in the Ottoman Empire and their control of the Straits, all in the hope of improving relations with Britain and isolating France".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges201168_50-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges201168-50"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>46<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> But Britain after 1838 was interested in preserving the integrity of the Ottoman Empire and rejected all Russian proposals. "The fall of the Ottoman Empire was not, however, a requirement of British policy in the East. A weak Ottoman state best suited British interests".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBadem201068_51-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBadem201068-51"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>47<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

### Immediate causes of war

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Crimean_War&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Immediate causes of war")\]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Napoleon3.PNG/250px-Napoleon3.PNG)

French Emperor [Napoleon III](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Napoleon_III "Napoleon III")

French Emperor [Napoleon III](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Napoleon_III "Napoleon III")'s ambition to restore France's grandeur<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>48<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> initiated the immediate chain of events that led to France and Britain declaring war on Russia on 27 and 28 March 1854, respectively. He pursued [Catholic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic "Catholic") support by asserting France's "sovereign authority" over the Christian population of [Palestine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palestine_\(region\) "Palestine (region)"),<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERoyle200019_53-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTERoyle200019-53"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>49<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> to the detriment of Russia<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010103_54-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010103-54"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>50<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> (the sponsor of [Eastern Orthodoxy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Christianity "Eastern Christianity")). To achieve that, he in May 1851 appointed [Charles, marquis de La Valette](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles,_marquis_de_La_Valette "Charles, marquis de La Valette"), a zealous leading member of the Catholic clericalists, as his ambassador to the [Sublime Porte](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sublime_Porte "Sublime Porte") of the Ottoman Empire.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges20107–9_55-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges20107%E2%80%939-55"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>51<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

Russia disputed that attempted change in authority. Referring to two previous treaties (one from 1757 and the [Treaty of Küçük Kaynarca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_K%C3%BC%C3%A7%C3%BCk_Kaynarca "Treaty of Küçük Kaynarca") from 1774), the Ottomans reversed their earlier decision, renounced the French treaty and declared that Russia was the protector of the [Orthodox Christians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Orthodox_Church "Eastern Orthodox Church") in the Ottoman Empire.

Napoleon III responded with a show of force by sending the [ship of the line](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ship_of_the_line "Ship of the line") *[Charlemagne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_ship_Charlemagne_\(1851\) "French ship Charlemagne (1851)")* to the Black Sea and thereby violated the London Straits Convention.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010104_56-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010104-56"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>52<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERoyle200019_53-1" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTERoyle200019-53"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>49<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The [gunboat diplomacy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gunboat_diplomacy "Gunboat diplomacy") show of force, together with money<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>, induced Ottoman Sultan [Abdülmecid I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abd%C3%BClmecid_I "Abdülmecid I") to accept a new treaty confirming France and the Catholic Church's supreme authority over Christian holy places, including the [Church of the Nativity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Church_of_the_Nativity "Church of the Nativity"), which had been held by the [Greek Orthodox Church](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_Orthodox_Church_of_Jerusalem "Greek Orthodox Church of Jerusalem").<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERoyle200020_57-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTERoyle200020-57"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>53<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

Tsar [Nicholas I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicholas_I_of_Russia "Nicholas I of Russia") then deployed his 4th and 5th Army Corps along the River [Danube](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Danube "Danube") in Wallachia, as a direct threat to the Ottoman lands south of the river. He had Foreign Minister Count [Karl Nesselrode](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karl_Nesselrode "Karl Nesselrode") undertake talks with the Ottomans. Nesselrode confided to Seymour:

> *\[The dispute over the holy places\]* had assumed a new character—that the acts of injustice towards the Greek church which it had been desired to prevent had been perpetrated and consequently that now the object must be to find a remedy for these wrongs. The success of French negotiations at Constantinople was to be ascribed solely to intrigue and violence—violence which had been supposed to be the [ultima ratio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ultima_ratio "Ultima ratio") of kings, being, it had been seen, the means which the present ruler of France was in the habit of employing in the first instance.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERoyle200021_58-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTERoyle200021-58"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>54<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/Count_Nesselrode.jpg/250px-Count_Nesselrode.jpg)

For forty years (1816–56) [Karl Nesselrode](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karl_Nesselrode "Karl Nesselrode") as foreign minister guided Russian policy

The agreement referred to by the French was in 1740.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTERoyle200018_59-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTERoyle200018-59"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>55<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> At present most historians (except for the new Russian Orthodox nationalists) accept that the question of the holy places was no more than a pretext for the Crimean War.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBadem201065_60-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBadem201065-60"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>56<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> As conflict emerged over the issue of the holy places, Nicholas I and Nesselrode began a diplomatic offensive, which they hoped would prevent either British or French interference in any conflict between Russia and the Ottomans and prevent both from forming an anti-Russian alliance.

Nicholas began courting Britain by means of conversations with Seymour in January and February 1853.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010105_61-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010105-61"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>57<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Nicholas insisted that he no longer wished to expand the Russian Empire<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010105_61-1" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010105-61"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>57<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> but that he had an obligation to the Christian communities in the Ottoman Empire.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010105_61-2" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010105-61"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>57<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> He next dispatched a highly-abrasive diplomat, [Prince Menshikov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander_Sergeyevich_Menshikov "Alexander Sergeyevich Menshikov"), on a special mission to the Ottoman Sublime Porte in February 1853. By previous treaties, the sultan had committed "to protect the (Eastern Orthodox) Christian religion and its churches". Menshikov demanded a Russian protectorate over all 12 million Orthodox Christians in the Ottoman Empire with control of the Orthodox Church's hierarchy. A compromise was reached regarding Orthodox access to the Holy Land, but the Sultan, strongly supported by the British ambassador, [Stratford Canning, 1st Viscount Stratford de Redcliffe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stratford_Canning,_1st_Viscount_Stratford_de_Redcliffe "Stratford Canning, 1st Viscount Stratford de Redcliffe"), rejected the most sweeping demands.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-62"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>58<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

Russian historian Vinogradov V.N. point out that Menshikov's demands did not go beyond the limits of previous treaties. "The agreement was reached on the administration of church rites of both clergy in respected temples and, secondly, that the tsar rejected the idea of expanding his right of patronage and, in fact, insisted on confirming the terms of the Kucuk-Kaynardzhiy treaty of 1774, which allowed giving advice to the Sultan, but did not oblige them to accept".<sup id="cite_ref-Vinogradov_21-1" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-Vinogradov-21"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

"By the early 1850s Stratford Canning had become far more than an ambassador or adviser to the Porte. The 'Great Elchi', or Great Ambassador, as he was known in Constantinople, had a direct influence on the policies of the Turkish government. (...) His presence was a source of deep resentment among the Sultan's ministers, who lived in terror of a personal visit from the dictatorial ambassador".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges201064_63-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges201064-63"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>59<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

Nicholas fumed at "the infernal dictatorship of this Redcliffe" whose name and political ascendancy at the Porte personified for him the whole Eastern Question,<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-64"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>60<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The British and the French sent in naval task forces to support the Ottomans, as Russia had prepared to seize the [Danubian Principalities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Danubian_Principalities "Danubian Principalities").<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010111–115_65-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010111%E2%80%93115-65"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>61<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

All the calculations of the Russian emperor turned out to be erroneous. Britain refused his proposals, it was not possible to prevent the Anglo-French rapprochement, Austria opposed his policy, the Ottoman Empire showed intransigence. On the contrary, a favourable situation was developing for Britain. Britain had great naval power and a powerful economy, but did not have a strong land army. The alliance with France, which had a strong land army, made it possible to strike at Russia. "With the help of French infantry, it was possible to overturn Russia's positions with one blow"<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-66"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>62<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

### First hostilities

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Crimean_War&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: First hostilities")\]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/The_Allied_Fleets_anchored_in_the_Bosphorus%2C_1853.jpg/250px-The_Allied_Fleets_anchored_in_the_Bosphorus%2C_1853.jpg)

British warships anchored in the [Bosphorus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bosporus "Bosporus"), late 1853; the prelude to the Crimean War. Painted by [Amedeo Preziosi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amedeo_Preziosi "Amedeo Preziosi").

In February 1853, the British government of Prime Minister [Lord Aberdeen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Hamilton-Gordon,_4th_Earl_of_Aberdeen "George Hamilton-Gordon, 4th Earl of Aberdeen") reappointed Lord Stratford as British ambassador to the Ottoman Empire.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010110_67-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010110-67"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>63<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Having resigned the ambassadorship in January, he had been replaced by [Colonel Rose](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hugh_Rose,_1st_Baron_Strathnairn "Hugh Rose, 1st Baron Strathnairn") as *[chargé d'affaires](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charg%C3%A9_d%27affaires "Chargé d'affaires")*. Lord Stratford then turned around, sailed back to Constantinople, arriving there on 5 April 1853 and convinced the Sultan there to reject the Russian treaty proposal as compromising Ottoman independence. The [Leader of the Opposition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leader_of_the_Opposition_\(United_Kingdom\) "Leader of the Opposition (United Kingdom)") in the British [House of Commons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/House_of_Commons_of_the_United_Kingdom "House of Commons of the United Kingdom"), [Benjamin Disraeli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benjamin_Disraeli "Benjamin Disraeli"), blamed Aberdeen's and Stratford's actions for making war inevitable, which started the process that would force the [Aberdeen government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aberdeen_ministry "Aberdeen ministry") to resign in January 1855 over the war.

Shortly after the Tsar had learned of the failure of Menshikov's diplomacy toward the end of June 1853, he sent armies under the commands of Field Marshal [Ivan Paskevich](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ivan_Paskevich "Ivan Paskevich") and General [Mikhail Gorchakov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mikhail_Dmitrievich_Gorchakov "Mikhail Dmitrievich Gorchakov") across the River [Prut](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prut "Prut") into the Ottoman-controlled [Danubian Principalities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Danubian_Principalities "Danubian Principalities") of Moldavia and Wallachia. Fewer than half of the 80,000 Russian soldiers who crossed the Prut in 1853 survived. By far, nearly all of the deaths would result from sickness, rather than action,<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010118–119_68-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010118%E2%80%93119-68"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>64<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> since the Russian Army still suffered from medical services that ranged from bad to none.

Russia had obtained recognition from the Ottoman Empire of the Tsar's role as special guardian of the Orthodox Christians in Moldavia and Wallachia. Russia now used the Sultan's failure to resolve the issue of the protection of the Christian sites in the Holy Land as a pretext for Russian occupation of those Danubian provinces. Nicholas believed that the European powers, especially Austria, would not object strongly to the annexation of a few neighbouring Ottoman provinces, especially since Russia had assisted Austria's efforts in suppressing the Hungarian Revolution in 1849.

The United Kingdom, hoping to maintain the Ottoman Empire as a bulwark against the expansion of Russian power in [Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asia "Asia"), sent a fleet to the Dardanelles, where it joined a fleet sent by France.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-69"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>65<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

#### Battle of Sinop

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Crimean_War&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Battle of Sinop")\]

Main article: [Battle of Sinop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Sinop "Battle of Sinop")

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/Battle_of_Sinop.jpg/250px-Battle_of_Sinop.jpg)

The Russian destruction of the Ottoman fleet at the [Battle of Sinop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Sinop "Battle of Sinop") on 30 November 1853 sparked the war (painting by [Ivan Aivazovsky](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ivan_Aivazovsky "Ivan Aivazovsky")).

The European powers continued to pursue diplomatic avenues. The representatives of the four Great Powers (the United Kingdom, France, Austria and [Prussia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Prussia "Kingdom of Prussia")) met in [Vienna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vienna "Vienna"), where they drafted a note, which they hoped would be acceptable to both the Russians and the Ottomans. The peace terms arrived at by the four powers at the Vienna Conference (1853) were delivered to the Russians by Austrian Foreign Minister [Count Karl von Buol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Count_Karl_Ferdinand_von_Buol "Count Karl Ferdinand von Buol") on 5 December 1853. The note met with the approval of Nicholas I, but Abdülmecid I rejected the proposal since he felt that the document's poor phrasing left it open to many different interpretations. The United Kingdom, France and Austria united in proposing amendments to mollify the Sultan, but the court of St. Petersburg ignored their suggestions.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010143_70-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010143-70"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>66<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The United Kingdom and France then set aside the idea of continuing negotiations, but Austria and Prussia did not believe that the rejection of the proposed amendments justified the abandonment of the diplomatic process.

On 23 November, a small Russian naval force discovered the Ottoman fleet harboured in [Sinop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinop,_Turkey "Sinop, Turkey") and began a blockade. Once the Russian blockade was reinforced, a squadron of 6 Russian ships of the line supported by 5 smaller warships, assaulted the harbour on 30 November 1853. During [Battle of Sinop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Sinop "Battle of Sinop"), the Russian squadron destroyed a patrol squadron of 11 Ottoman warships—mostly frigates—while they were anchored in port under defence of the onshore artillery garrison.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETucker2009[[Category:Wikipedia_articles_needing_page_number_citations_from_November_2022]]<sup_class=&quot;noprint_Inline-Template_&quot;_style=&quot;white-space:nowrap;&quot;>&amp;#91;<i>[[Wikipedia:Citing_sources|<span_title=&quot;This_citation_requires_a_reference_to_the_specific_page_or_range_of_pages_in_which_the_material_appears.&amp;#32;(November_2022)&quot;>page&amp;nbsp;needed</span>]]</i>&amp;#93;</sup>_71-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTETucker2009[[Category:Wikipedia_articles_needing_page_number_citations_from_November_2022]]%3Csup_class=%22noprint_Inline-Template_%22_style=%22white-space:nowrap;%22%3E&amp;#91;%3Ci%3E[[Wikipedia:Citing_sources|%3Cspan_title=%22This_citation_requires_a_reference_to_the_specific_page_or_range_of_pages_in_which_the_material_appears.&amp;#32;(November_2022)%22%3Epage&amp;nbsp;needed%3C/span%3E]]%3C/i%3E&amp;#93;%3C/sup%3E-71"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>67<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The Ottoman fleet suffered a crushing defeat. The Russian victory in the naval battle in Sinope was called "the massacre of Sinope".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEMarriott1917234_72-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMarriott1917234-72"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>68<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Although Russia and the Ottoman Empire were already at war, and there was no evidence of Russian atrocities, the phrase was used as propaganda in the West.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-73"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>69<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The press in both United Kingdom and France used Sinop as the *[casus belli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Casus_belli "Casus belli")* ("cause of war") to shape the public opinion in favour of war against Russia. By 28 March 1854, after Russia ignored an Anglo-French ultimatum to withdraw from the Danubian Principalities, the United Kingdom and France had both declared war.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_74-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/:0-74"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>70<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-75"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>71<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

### Dardanelles

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Crimean_War&action=edit&section=7 "Edit section: Dardanelles")\]

Britain was concerned about Russian activity and Sir [John Burgoyne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Fox_Burgoyne "John Fox Burgoyne"), a senior advisor to Lord Aberdeen, urged for the [Dardanelles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dardanelles "Dardanelles") to be occupied and works of sufficient strength to be built to block any Russian move to capture Constantinople and gain access to the Mediterranean. The [Corps of Royal Engineers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corps_of_Royal_Engineers "Corps of Royal Engineers") sent men to the Dardanelles, and Burgoyne went to Paris and met with the British ambassador and the French emperor. [Lord Cowley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henry_Wellesley,_1st_Earl_Cowley "Henry Wellesley, 1st Earl Cowley") wrote on 8 February to Burgoyne, "Your visit to Paris has produced a visible change in the Emperor's views, and he is making every preparation for a land expedition in case the last attempt at negotiation should break down".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPorter1889411_76-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPorter1889411-76"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>72<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

Burgoyne and his team of engineers inspected and surveyed the Dardanelles area in February. They were fired on by Russian riflemen when they went to [Varna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Varna,_Bulgaria "Varna, Bulgaria"). A team of [sappers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sapper "Sapper") arrived in March, and major building works commenced on a seven-mile line of defence, which was designed to block the [Gallipoli Peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gallipoli_Peninsula "Gallipoli Peninsula"). French sappers worked on half of the line, which was finished in May.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPorter1889412_77-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPorter1889412-77"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>73<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

### Peace attempts

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Crimean_War&action=edit&section=8 "Edit section: Peace attempts")\]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Valley_of_the_Shadow_of_Death%2C_2.jpg/250px-Valley_of_the_Shadow_of_Death%2C_2.jpg)

*[Valley of the Shadow of Death](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Valley_of_the_Shadow_of_Death_\(Roger_Fenton\) "Valley of the Shadow of Death (Roger Fenton)")*, by Roger Fenton, one of the most famous pictures of the Crimean War<sup id="cite_ref-78" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-78"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>74<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

Nicholas felt that because of Russian assistance in suppressing the [Hungarian Revolution of 1848](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hungarian_Revolution_of_1848 "Hungarian Revolution of 1848"), Austria would side with him or at the very least remain neutral. Austria, however, felt threatened by the Russian troops in the Balkans. On 27 February 1854, the United Kingdom and France demanded the withdrawal of Russian forces from the principalities. Austria supported them and, without declaring war on Russia, refused to guarantee its neutrality. Russia's rejection of the ultimatum proved to be the justification used by Britain and France to enter the war.

Russia soon withdrew its troops from the Danubian Principalities, which were then occupied by Austria for the duration of the war.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEArnold2002[httpsbooksgooglecombooksid_UreS--MoD0CpgPA13_13]_79-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEArnold2002[httpsbooksgooglecombooksid_UreS--MoD0CpgPA13_13]-79"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>75<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> That removed the original grounds for war, but the British and the French continued with hostilities. Determined to address the Eastern Question by putting an end to the Russian threat to the Ottomans, the allies in August 1854 proposed the "Four Points" for ending the conflict in addition to the Russian withdrawal:

- Russia was to give up its protectorate over the Danubian Principalities.
- The Danube was to be opened up to foreign commerce.
- The [Straits Convention of 1841](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/London_Straits_Convention "London Straits Convention"), which allowed only Ottoman and Russian warships in the Black Sea, was to be revised.
- Russia was to abandon any claim granting it the right to interfere in Ottoman affairs on behalf of Orthodox Christians.

Those points, particularly the third, would require clarification through negotiations, which Russia refused. The allies, including Austria, therefore agreed that Britain and France should take further military action to prevent further Russian aggression against the Ottomans. Britain and France agreed on the invasion of Crimea as the first step.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESmall200723,_31_80-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTESmall200723,_31-80"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>76<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

### Danube campaign

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Crimean_War&action=edit&section=10 "Edit section: Danube campaign")\]

See also: [Wallachian Revolution of 1848](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wallachian_Revolution_of_1848 "Wallachian Revolution of 1848"), [Moldavian Revolution of 1848](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moldavian_Revolution_of_1848 "Moldavian Revolution of 1848"), and [Convention of Balta Liman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Convention_of_Balta_Liman "Convention of Balta Liman")

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Ottoman_ship_of_the_line_Mahmudiye.png/250px-Ottoman_ship_of_the_line_Mahmudiye.png)

*[Mahmudiye](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mahmudiye_\(ship\) "Mahmudiye (ship)")* (1829) participated in numerous important naval battles, including the [Siege of Sevastopol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siege_of_Sevastopol_\(1854%E2%80%931855\) "Siege of Sevastopol (1854–1855)")

The Danube campaign opened when the Russians occupied the Danubian Principalities of Moldavia and Wallachia in July 1853,<sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-81"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>77<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> which brought their forces to the north bank of the River Danube. In response, the Ottoman Empire also moved its forces up to the river, establishing strongholds at [Vidin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vidin "Vidin") in the west and [Silistra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silistra "Silistra")<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010172–184_82-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010172%E2%80%93184-82"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>78<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> in the east, near the mouth of the Danube. The Ottoman move up the River Danube was also of concern to the Austrians, who moved forces into [Transylvania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transylvania "Transylvania") in response. However, the Austrians had begun to fear the Russians more than the Ottomans. Indeed, like the British, the Austrians were now coming to see that an intact Ottoman Empire was necessary as a bulwark against the Russians. Accordingly, Austria resisted Russian diplomatic attempts to join the war but remained neutral during the Crimean War.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETaylor1954[httpsarchiveorgdetailsdlibengal1068913016pagen106_pp._64]–81_83-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTETaylor1954[httpsarchiveorgdetailsdlibengal1068913016pagen106_pp._64]%E2%80%9381-83"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>79<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

After the Ottoman ultimatum in September 1853, forces under Ottoman General [Omar Pasha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Omar_Pasha "Omar Pasha") crossed the Danube at Vidin and captured [Calafat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calafat "Calafat") in October 1853. Simultaneously, in the east, the Ottomans crossed the Danube at Silistra and attacked the Russians at [Oltenița](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olteni%C8%9Ba "Oltenița"). The resulting [Battle of Oltenița](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Olteni%C8%9Ba "Battle of Oltenița") was the first engagement since the declaration of war. The Russians counterattacked but were beaten back.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBadem2010101–109_84-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBadem2010101%E2%80%93109-84"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>80<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> On 31 December 1853, the Ottoman forces at Calafat moved against the Russian force at Chetatea or [Cetate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cetate,_Dolj "Cetate, Dolj"), a small village nine miles north of Calafat, and engaged it on 6 January 1854. The battle began when the Russians made a move to recapture Calafat. Most of the [heavy fighting took place in and around Chetatea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Cetate "Battle of Cetate") until the Russians were driven out of the village. Despite the setback at Chetatea, Russian forces on 28 January 1854 laid [siege to Calafat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siege_of_Calafat "Siege of Calafat"). The siege would continue until May 1854 when it was lifted by the Russians. The Ottomans would also later beat the Russians in battle at [Caracal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caracal,_Romania "Caracal, Romania").<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010130–143_85-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010130%E2%80%93143-85"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>81<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

In early 1854, the Russians again advanced by crossing the River Danube into the Turkish province of [Dobruja](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dobruja "Dobruja"). By April 1854, the Russians had reached the lines of [Trajan's Wall](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trajan%27s_Wall "Trajan's Wall"), where they were finally halted. In the centre, the Russian forces crossed the Danube and laid [siege to Silistra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siege_of_Silistria_\(1854\) "Siege of Silistria (1854)") from 14 April with 60,000 troops. The defenders had 15,000 troops and supplies for three months.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPorter1889415_86-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPorter1889415-86"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>82<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The siege was lifted on 23 June 1854.<sup id="cite_ref-87" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-87"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>83<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The British and the French could not then take the field for lack of equipment.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPorter1889415_86-1" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPorter1889415-86"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>82<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/%D0%9E%D1%81%D0%B0%D0%B4%D0%B0_%D0%A1%D0%B8%D0%BB%D0%B8%D1%81%D1%82%D1%80%D0%B8%D0%B8_1._%D0%92%D0%B8%D0%BB%D0%BB%D0%B5%D0%B2%D0%B0%D0%BB%D1%8C%D0%B4%D0%B5.jpg/250px-%D0%9E%D1%81%D0%B0%D0%B4%D0%B0_%D0%A1%D0%B8%D0%BB%D0%B8%D1%81%D1%82%D1%80%D0%B8%D0%B8_1._%D0%92%D0%B8%D0%BB%D0%BB%D0%B5%D0%B2%D0%B0%D0%BB%D1%8C%D0%B4%D0%B5.jpg)

The Siege of Silistra

In the west, the Russians were dissuaded from attacking Vidin by the presence of the Austrian forces, which had swollen to 280,000 men. On 28 May 1854, a protocol of the Vienna Conference was signed by Austria and Russia. One of the aims of the Russian advance had been to encourage the Orthodox Christian [Serbs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serbs "Serbs") and [Bulgarians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgarians "Bulgarians") who were living under Ottoman rule to rebel. When the Russian troops crossed the River [Prut](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prut "Prut") into Moldavia, the Orthodox Christians showed no interest in rising up against the Ottomans.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010131,_137_88-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010131,_137-88"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>84<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Adding to Nicholas I's worries was the concern that Austria would enter the war against the Russians and attack his armies on the western flank. Indeed, after attempting to mediate a peaceful settlement between Russia and the Ottomans, the Austrians entered the war on the side of the Ottomans with an attack against the Russians in the Danubian Principalities which threatened to cut off the Russian supply lines. Accordingly, the Russians were forced to raise the siege of Silistra on 23 June 1854 and to begin abandoning the principalities.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010185_89-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010185-89"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>85<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The lifting of the siege reduced the threat of a Russian advance into Bulgaria.

In June 1854, the Allied expeditionary force landed at [Varna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Varna,_Bulgaria "Varna, Bulgaria"), a city on the Black Sea's western coast, but made little advance from its base there.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010175–176_90-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010175%E2%80%93176-90"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>86<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> [Karl Marx](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karl_Marx "Karl Marx") was noted to have quipped that "there they are, the French doing nothing and the British helping them as fast as possible".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETroubetzkoy2006192_91-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTETroubetzkoy2006192-91"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>87<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> In July 1854, the Ottomans, under Omar Pasha, crossed the Danube into Wallachia and on 7 July 1854 engaged the Russians in the city of [Giurgiu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giurgiu "Giurgiu") and conquered it. The capture of Giurgiu by the Ottomans immediately threatened [Bucharest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bucharest "Bucharest") in Wallachia with capture by the same Ottoman army. On 26 July 1854, Nicholas I, responding to an Austrian ultimatum, ordered the withdrawal of Russian troops from the principalities. Also, in late July 1854, following up on the Russian retreat, the French staged an expedition against the Russian forces still in Dobruja, but it was a failure.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010188–190_92-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010188%E2%80%93190-92"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>88<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

By then, the Russian withdrawal was complete, except for the fortress towns of northern Dobruja, and Russia's place in the principalities was taken by the Austrians as a neutral peacekeeping force.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010189_93-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010189-93"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>89<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> There was little further action on that front after late 1854, and in September, the allied force boarded ships at Varna to invade [Crimea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crimea "Crimea").<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010198_94-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010198-94"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>90<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

### Black Sea theatre

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Crimean_War&action=edit&section=11 "Edit section: Black Sea theatre")\]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Turkish_troops_storming_Fort_Shefketil_%28cropped%29.jpg/250px-Turkish_troops_storming_Fort_Shefketil_%28cropped%29.jpg)

Turkish troops storming [Fort Shefketil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shekvetili "Shekvetili")

The naval operations of the Crimean War commenced with the dispatch in mid-1853 of the French and the British fleets to the Black Sea region, to support the Ottomans and to dissuade the Russians from encroachment. By June 1853, both fleets had been stationed at [Besikas Bay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Be%C5%9Fik_Bay,_%C3%87anakkale "Beşik Bay, Çanakkale"), outside the Dardanelles. With the Russian occupation of the Danube Principalities in July 1853, they moved to the Bosphorus, and on 3 January 1854, they entered the Black Sea.<sup id="cite_ref-Britannica_13-2" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-Britannica-13"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

Meanwhile, the Russian [Black Sea Fleet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Sea_Fleet "Black Sea Fleet") operated against Ottoman coastal traffic between Constantinople and the Caucasus ports, and the Ottoman fleet sought to protect the supply line. The clash came on 30 November 1853, when a Russian fleet attacked an Ottoman force in the harbour at [Sinop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinop,_Turkey "Sinop, Turkey") and destroyed it at the Battle of Sinop. The battle outraged British public opinion, which called for war.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEArnold2002[httpsbooksgooglecombooksid_UreS--MoD0CpgPR95_95]_95-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEArnold2002[httpsbooksgooglecombooksid_UreS--MoD0CpgPR95_95]-95"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>91<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> There was little additional naval action until March 1854, when after the declaration of war, the British frigate [HMS *Furious*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HMS_Furious_\(1850\) "HMS Furious (1850)") was fired on outside [Odessa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Odessa "Odessa") Harbour. In response an Anglo-French fleet [bombarded the port](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1854_bombardment_of_Odessa "1854 bombardment of Odessa") and caused much damage to the town. To show support for the Ottomans after the Battle of Sinop, on 22 December 1853, the Anglo-French squadron entered the Black Sea and the steamship HMS *Retribution* approached the [Port of Sevastopol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Port_of_Sevastopol "Port of Sevastopol"). Its commander received an ultimatum not to allow any ships in the Black Sea.

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/Debarquement_des_Fran%C3%A7ais_et_des_Anglais_dans_la_baie_de_Kalamita_le_14_septembre_1854.jpg/250px-Debarquement_des_Fran%C3%A7ais_et_des_Anglais_dans_la_baie_de_Kalamita_le_14_septembre_1854.jpg)

The French landing near [Yevpatoria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yevpatoria "Yevpatoria"), Crimea, 1854

In June, the fleets transported the Allied expeditionary forces to Varna to support the Ottoman operations on the Danube. In September they again transported the armies, this time to Crimea. The Russian fleet then declined to engage the allies but preferred to maintain a "[fleet in being](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fleet_in_being "Fleet in being")", a strategy that failed when Sevastopol, the main port and the base of most of the Black Sea fleet, came under siege. The Russians were reduced to scuttling their warships as [blockships](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blockship "Blockship") after they had stripped them of their guns and men to reinforce batteries on shore. During the siege, the Russians lost four 110- or 120-gun, three-decker ships of the line, twelve 84-gun two-deckers and four 60-gun [frigates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frigate "Frigate") in the Black Sea, as well as a large number of smaller vessels. During the rest of the campaign, the allied fleets remained in control of the Black Sea and ensured that the various fronts were kept supplied.

In May 1855, the allies successfully invaded [Kerch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kerch "Kerch") and [operated against Taganrog](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siege_of_Taganrog "Siege of Taganrog") in the [Sea of Azov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_of_Azov "Sea of Azov"). In September, they moved against Russian installations in the [Dnieper](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dnieper "Dnieper") estuary by [attacking Kinburn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Kinburn_\(1855\) "Battle of Kinburn (1855)") in the first use of [ironclad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ironclad "Ironclad") ships in naval warfare.

### Crimean campaign

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Crimean_War&action=edit&section=12 "Edit section: Crimean campaign")\]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Russo-British_skirmish_during_Crimean_War.png/250px-Russo-British_skirmish_during_Crimean_War.png)

Russo-British skirmish during the Crimean War. By [Harry Payne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harry_Payne_\(artist\) "Harry Payne (artist)")

The Russians evacuated Wallachia and Moldavia in late July 1854. Therefore, the immediate cause of war had now been withdrawn, and the war might have then ended.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010192_96-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010192-96"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>92<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> However, war fever among the public in both Britain and France had been whipped up by the press in both countries to the degree that politicians found it untenable to propose immediately ending the war. The coalition government of [George Hamilton-Gordon, 4th Earl of Aberdeen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Hamilton-Gordon,_4th_Earl_of_Aberdeen "George Hamilton-Gordon, 4th Earl of Aberdeen"), fell on 30 January 1855 on a no-confidence vote, as Parliament voted to appoint a committee to investigate the mismanagement of the war.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010311_97-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010311-97"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>93<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

French and British officers and engineers were sent on 20 July on *[Fury](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HMS_Fury_\(1845\) "HMS Fury (1845)")*, a wooden *Bulldog*\-class paddle sloop, to survey the harbour of Sevastopol and the coast near it. They managed to get close to the harbour mouth to inspect the formidable batteries. Returning, they reported that they believed that 15,000–20,000 troops were encamped.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPorter1889421_98-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPorter1889421-98"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>94<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Ships were prepared to transport horses, and siege equipment was both manufactured and imported.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPorter1889422_99-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPorter1889422-99"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>95<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

The Crimean campaign opened in September 1854. In seven columns, 360 ships sailed, each steamer towing two sailing ships.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPorter1889422_99-1" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPorter1889422-99"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>95<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Anchoring on 13 September in the bay of [Yevpatoria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yevpatoria "Yevpatoria"), the town surrendered, and 500 marines landed to occupy it. The town and the bay would provide a fallback position in case of disaster.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010201_100-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010201-100"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>96<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The ships then sailed east to make the landing of the allied expeditionary force on the sandy beaches of [Kalamita Bay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kalamita_Bay "Kalamita Bay"), on the south-west coast of Crimea. The landing surprised the Russians, as they had expected a landing at [Katcha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kacha,_Sevastopol "Kacha, Sevastopol"). The last-minute change proved that Russia had known the original campaign plan. There was no sign of the enemy and so all of the invading troops landed on 14 September 1854. It took another four days to land all of the stores, equipment, horses and artillery.

The landing took place north of Sevastopol and so the Russians had arrayed their army in expectation of a direct attack. The allies advanced and on the morning of 20 September came up to the River [Alma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alma_\(Crimea\) "Alma (Crimea)") and engaged the Russian Army. The Russian position was strong, but after three hours,<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPorter1889424_101-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPorter1889424-101"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>97<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> the allied frontal attack had driven the Russians out of their dug-in positions with losses of 6,000 men. The [Battle of the Alma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_the_Alma "Battle of the Alma") resulted in 3,300 Allied losses. Failing to pursue the retreating forces was one of many strategic errors made during the war, and the Russians themselves noted that if the allies had pressed south that day, they would have easily captured Sevastopol.

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/29/La_bataille_de_l%27Alma_en_1854.jpg/250px-La_bataille_de_l%27Alma_en_1854.jpg)

The Battle of the Alma

Believing the northern approaches to the city too well defended, especially because of the presence of a large [star fort](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Star_fort "Star fort") and the city being on the south side of [Sevastopol Bay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sevastopol_Bay "Sevastopol Bay"), Sir John Burgoyne, the engineer advisor, recommended for the allies attack to Sevastopol from the south. The joint commanders, [Raglan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FitzRoy_Somerset,_1st_Baron_Raglan "FitzRoy Somerset, 1st Baron Raglan") and [Saint-Arnaud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jacques_Leroy_de_Saint-Arnaud "Jacques Leroy de Saint-Arnaud"), agreed.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPorter1889426_102-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPorter1889426-102"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>98<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> On 25 September, the whole army began to march southeast and encircled the city from the south after it had established port facilities at [Balaclava](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balaklava "Balaklava") for the British and at [Kamiesch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kamiesch "Kamiesch") ([Russian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_language "Russian language"): Камышовая бухта, [romanized](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romanization_of_Russian "Romanization of Russian"): *Kamyshovaya bukhta*) for the French. The Russians retreated into the city.<sup id="cite_ref-103" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-103"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>99<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-104" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-104"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>100<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

The Allied armies moved without problems to the south, and the heavy artillery was brought ashore with batteries and connecting trenches built. By 10 October, some batteries were ready, and by 17 October, when the bombardment commenced—126 guns were firing, 53 of them French.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPorter1889430_105-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPorter1889430-105"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>101<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The fleet meanwhile engaged the shore batteries. The British bombardment worked better than that of the French, who had smaller-calibre guns. The fleet suffered high casualties during the day. The British wanted to attack that afternoon, but the French wanted to defer the attack.

A postponement was agreed, but on the next day, the French were still not ready. By 19 October the Russians had transferred some heavy guns to the southern defences and had outgunned the allies.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPorter1889431_106-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPorter1889431-106"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>102<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

Reinforcements for the Russians gave them the courage to send out probing attacks. The Allied lines, beginning to suffer from cholera as early as September, were stretched. The French, on the west, had less to do than the British on the east, with their siege lines and the large nine-mile open wing back to their supply base on the south coast.

### Battle of Balaclava

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Crimean_War&action=edit&section=13 "Edit section: Battle of Balaclava")\]

Main article: [Battle of Balaclava](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Balaclava "Battle of Balaclava")

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Relief_of_the_Light_Brigade.png/250px-Relief_of_the_Light_Brigade.png)

British cavalry charging against Russian forces at Balaclava

A large Russian assault on the allied supply base to the southeast at Balaclava was rebuffed on 25 October 1854.<sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 521–527">: 521–527 </span></sup>  The [Battle of Balaclava](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Balaclava "Battle of Balaclava") is remembered in Britain for the actions of two British units. At the start of the battle, a large body of Russian cavalry charged the [93rd Highlanders](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/93rd_Highlanders "93rd Highlanders"), who were posted north of the village of [Kadikoi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kadikoi "Kadikoi"). Commanding them was Sir [Colin Campbell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colin_Campbell,_1st_Baron_Clyde "Colin Campbell, 1st Baron Clyde"). Rather than "[form square](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infantry_square "Infantry square")", the traditional method of repelling cavalry, Campbell took the risky decision to have his Highlanders form a single line two men deep. Campbell had seen the effectiveness of the new [Minié rifles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mini%C3%A9_rifle "Minié rifle") with which his troops were armed at the Battle of Alma, a month earlier, and he was confident that his men could beat back the Russians. His tactics succeeded.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGreenwood2015ch._8_107-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGreenwood2015ch._8-107"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>103<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> From up on the ridge to the west, the *[Times](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Times "The Times")* correspondent [William Howard Russell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Howard_Russell "William Howard Russell") saw the Highlanders as a "thin red streak topped with steel", a phrase which soon became the "[Thin Red Line](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Thin_Red_Line_\(Battle_of_Balaclava\) "The Thin Red Line (Battle of Balaclava)")".<sup id="cite_ref-108" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-108"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>104<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

Soon afterward, a Russian cavalry movement was countered by the [Heavy Brigade](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charge_of_the_Heavy_Brigade "Charge of the Heavy Brigade"), which charged and fought hand to hand until the Russians retreated. That caused a more widespread Russian retreat, including a number of their artillery units. After the local commanders had failed to take advantage of the retreat, Lord Raglan sent out orders to move up and to prevent the withdrawal of naval guns from the recently captured redoubts on the heights. Raglan could see those guns because of his position on the hill. In the valley, that view was obstructed, and the wrong guns were in sight to the left. The local commanders ignored the demands, which led to the British [aide-de-camp](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aide-de-camp "Aide-de-camp"), Captain [Louis Nolan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louis_Nolan "Louis Nolan"), personally delivering the quickly-written and confusing order to attack the artillery. When [Lord Lucan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Bingham,_3rd_Earl_of_Lucan "George Bingham, 3rd Earl of Lucan") questioned to which guns the order referred, the aide-de-camp pointed to the first Russian battery that he could see and allegedly said "There is your enemy, there are your guns", because of his obstructed view, which were wrong. Lucan then passed the order to the [Earl of Cardigan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Brudenell,_7th_Earl_of_Cardigan "James Brudenell, 7th Earl of Cardigan"), which resulted in the [Charge of the Light Brigade](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charge_of_the_Light_Brigade "Charge of the Light Brigade").

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Robert_Gibb_-_The_Thin_Red_Line.jpg/250px-Robert_Gibb_-_The_Thin_Red_Line.jpg)

*[The Thin Red Line](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Thin_Red_Line_\(Battle_of_Balaclava\) "The Thin Red Line (Battle of Balaclava)")* at the Battle of Balaclava, where the [93rd Sutherland Highlanders](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/93rd_\(Sutherland_Highlanders\)_Regiment_of_Foot "93rd (Sutherland Highlanders) Regiment of Foot") held off Russian cavalry.

In that charge, Cardigan formed up his unit and charged the length of the Valley of the Balaclava, under fire from Russian batteries in the hills. The charge of the Light Brigade caused 278 casualties of the 700-man unit. The Light Brigade was memorialised in the famous poem by [Alfred, Lord Tennyson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alfred,_Lord_Tennyson "Alfred, Lord Tennyson"), "[The Charge of the Light Brigade](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Charge_of_the_Light_Brigade_\(poem\) "The Charge of the Light Brigade (poem)")". Although traditionally, the charge of the Light Brigade was looked upon as a glorious but wasted sacrifice of good men and horses, recent historians believe that the charge of the Light Brigade succeeded in at least some of its objectives.<sup id="cite_ref-109" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-109"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>105<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific page or range of pages in which the material appears. (January 2022)">page&nbsp;needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The aim of any cavalry charge is to scatter the enemy's lines and frighten the enemy off the battlefield. The Charge of the Light Brigade so unnerved the Russian cavalry, which had been routed by the Charge of the Heavy Brigade, that the Russians were set to full-scale flight.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010252_110-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010252-110"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>106<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESmall2007_111-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTESmall2007-111"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>107<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

The shortage of men led to the failure of the British and the French to follow up on the Battle of Balaclava, which led directly to the much bloodier [Battle of Inkerman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Inkerman "Battle of Inkerman"). On 5 November 1854, the Russians attempted to raise the siege at Sevastopol with an attack against the allies, which resulted in another allied victory.<sup id="cite_ref-112" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-112"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>108<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific page or range of pages in which the material appears. (January 2022)">page&nbsp;needed</span></a></i>]</sup>

### Winter of 1854–1855

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Crimean_War&action=edit&section=14 "Edit section: Winter of 1854–1855")\]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Weller_Siege_of_Sebastopol_1854-1855.jpg/250px-Weller_Siege_of_Sebastopol_1854-1855.jpg)

Historical map showing the territory between Balaclava and Sevastopol at the time of the Siege of Sevastopol

Winter weather and a deteriorating supply of troops and [materiel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Materiel "Materiel") on both sides led to a halt in ground operations. Sevastopol remained invested by the allies, whose armies were hemmed in by the [Imperial Russian Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imperial_Russian_Army "Imperial Russian Army") in the interior. On 14 November, the "[Balaklava Storm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Storm_of_1854 "Great Storm of 1854")", a major weather event, sank 30 allied transport ships,<sup id="cite_ref-113" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-113"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>109<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> including [HMS *Prince*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HMS_Prince_\(1854\) "HMS Prince (1854)"), which was carrying a cargo of winter clothing.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPorter1889435_114-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPorter1889435-114"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>110<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

The storm and the heavy traffic caused the road from the coast to the troops to disintegrate into a quagmire, which required engineers to devote most of their time to its repair, including by quarrying stone. A [tramway](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plateway "Plateway") was ordered and arrived in January with a civilian engineering crew, but it took until March before it had become sufficiently advanced to be of any appreciable value.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPorter1889439_115-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPorter1889439-115"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>111<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> An [electrical telegraph](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electrical_telegraph "Electrical telegraph") was also ordered, but the frozen ground delayed its installation until March, when communications from the base port of Balaklava to the British HQ was established. The [pipe-and-cable-laying plough](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pipe-and-cable-laying_plough "Pipe-and-cable-laying plough") failed because of the hard frozen soil, but nevertheless 21 miles (34 km) of cable were laid.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPorter1889449_116-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPorter1889449-116"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>112<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

The troops suffered greatly from cold and sickness, and the shortage of fuel led them to start dismantling their defensive [gabions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gabion "Gabion") and [fascines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fascine "Fascine").<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPorter1889442_117-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPorter1889442-117"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>113<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> In February 1855, the Russians attacked the allied base at Eupatoria, where an Ottoman army had built up and was threatening Russian supply routes. The Russians were defeated at the [Battle of Eupatoria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Eupatoria "Battle of Eupatoria"),<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010321–322_118-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010321%E2%80%93322-118"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>114<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> leading to a change in their command.

The strain of directing the war had taken its toll on the health of Tsar Nicholas. Full of remorse for the disasters that he had caused, he caught pneumonia and died on 2 March.<sup id="cite_ref-Radzinsky2005_119-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-Radzinsky2005-119"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>115<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 96">: 96 </span></sup> 

### Siege of Sevastopol

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Crimean_War&action=edit&section=15 "Edit section: Siege of Sevastopol")\]

Main article: [Siege of Sevastopol (1854–1855)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siege_of_Sevastopol_\(1854%E2%80%931855\) "Siege of Sevastopol (1854–1855)")

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/52/Crimea_Sevastopol_Istorychny_boulevard_Memorial_complex-55.jpg/250px-Crimea_Sevastopol_Istorychny_boulevard_Memorial_complex-55.jpg)

*[The Siege of Sevastopol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siege_of_Sevastopol_\(panorama\) "Siege of Sevastopol (panorama)")* panorama by [Franz Roubaud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_Roubaud "Franz Roubaud")

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Siege_of_Sevastopol_1855.jpg/220px-Siege_of_Sevastopol_1855.jpg)

*Siege of Sevastopol 1855* by Grigoryi Shukaev

The allies had had time to consider the problem, and the French were brought around to agree that the key to the defence was the [Malakoff](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malakhov_Kurgan "Malakhov Kurgan").<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPorter1889441_120-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPorter1889441-120"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>116<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Emphasis of the siege at Sevastopol shifted to the British left against the fortifications on Malakoff Hill.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010339_121-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010339-121"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>117<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> In March, there was fighting by the French over a new fort being built by the Russians at [Mamelon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mamelon_\(fort\) "Mamelon (fort)"), on a hill in front of the Malakoff. Several weeks of fighting resulted in little change in the front line, and the Mamelon remained in Russian hands.

In April 1855, the allies staged a second all-out bombardment, which led to an artillery duel with the Russian guns, but no ground assault followed.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010340–341_122-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010340%E2%80%93341-122"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>118<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

On 24 May 1855, 60 ships, containing 7,000 French, 5,000 Turkish and 3,000 British troops, set off for a raid on the city of [Kerch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kerch "Kerch"), east of Sevastopol, in an attempt to open another front in Crimea and to cut off Russian supplies.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010344_123-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010344-123"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>119<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> When the allies landed the force at Kerch, the plan was to outflank the Russian Army. The landings were successful, but the force made little progress thereafter.

Many more artillery pieces had arrived and had been dug into batteries. The first general assault of Sevastopol took place on 18 June 1855. There is a legend that the assault was scheduled for that date in favour of Napoleon III in the 40th anniversary of the [Battle of Waterloo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Waterloo "Battle of Waterloo"), but the legend is not confirmed by historians.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETarle1950367_124-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTETarle1950367-124"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>120<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> However, the appearance of such a legend is undoubtedly symptomatic since the war in France was understood as a certain revanche for the [defeat of 1812](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_invasion_of_Russia "French invasion of Russia").

In June, a third bombardment was followed after two days by a successful attack on the Mamelon, but a follow-up [assault on the Malakoff](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Malakoff "Battle of Malakoff") failed with heavy losses. Meanwhile, the garrison commander, Admiral [Pavel Nakhimov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pavel_Nakhimov "Pavel Nakhimov"), fell on 30 June 1855,<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010378_125-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010378-125"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>121<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> and Raglan died on 28 June.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPorter1889460_126-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPorter1889460-126"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>122<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Losses in those battles were so great that by agreement of military opponents short-term truces for removal of corpses were signed (these truces were described in the work of [Leo Tolstoy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leo_Tolstoy "Leo Tolstoy") "[Sevastopol Sketches](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sevastopol_Sketches "Sevastopol Sketches")"). The assault was beaten back with heavy casualties and in an undoubted victory for Russia. It is worth mentioning that the Russian [Siege of Sevastopol (panorama)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siege_of_Sevastopol_\(panorama\) "Siege of Sevastopol (panorama)") depicts the moment of the assault of Sevastopol on 18 June 1855.

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/Attaque_du_mamelon_vert_et_des_ouvrages_blancs%2C_7_juin_1855.jpg/250px-Attaque_du_mamelon_vert_et_des_ouvrages_blancs%2C_7_juin_1855.jpg)

French [zouaves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zouave "Zouave") attack Russian positions in the Battle of Malakoff

In August, the Russians again made an attack towards the base at Balaclava, which was defended by the French, newly arrived [Sardinian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crimean_War_order_of_battle:_Kingdom_of_Sardinia "Crimean War order of battle: Kingdom of Sardinia") and Ottoman troops.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPorter1889461_127-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPorter1889461-127"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>123<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The resulting [Battle of the Chernaya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_the_Chernaya "Battle of the Chernaya") was a defeat for the Russians, who suffered heavy casualties.

For months, each side had been building forward rifle pits and defensive positions, which resulted in many skirmishes. Artillery fire aimed to gain superiority over the enemy guns.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPorter1889450–462_128-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPorter1889450%E2%80%93462-128"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>124<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The final assault was made on 5 September, when another French bombardment (the sixth) was followed by an assault by the [French Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Army "French Army") on 8 September and resulted in the French capture of the Malakoff fort. The Russians failed to retake it and their defences collapsed. Meanwhile, the British assaulted the [Great Redan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_the_Great_Redan "Battle of the Great Redan"), a Russian defensive battlement just south of the city of Sevastopol, a position that had been attacked repeatedly for months. Whether the British captured the Redan remains in dispute: Russian historians recognise only the loss of the Malakhov Kurgan, a key point of defence, claiming that all other positions were retained.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETarle1950462_129-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTETarle1950462-129"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>125<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> What is agreed is that the Russians abandoned the positions, blew up their powder magazines and retreated to the north. The city finally fell on 9 September 1855, after a 337-day-long siege.<sup id="cite_ref-Radzinsky2005_119-1" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-Radzinsky2005-119"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>115<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page / location: 106">: 106 </span></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-130" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-130"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>126<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

Both sides were now exhausted, and no further military operations were launched in Crimea before the onset of winter. The main objective of the siege was the destruction of the Russian fleet and docks and took place over the winter. On 28 February, multiple mines blew up the five docks, the canal, and three locks.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEPorter1889471_131-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEPorter1889471-131"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>127<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

### Azov campaign

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Crimean_War&action=edit&section=16 "Edit section: Azov campaign")\]

Main article: [Siege of Taganrog](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siege_of_Taganrog "Siege of Taganrog")

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Simpson_Disembarkation_of_the_expedition_to_Kertch_at_Kamish_Bournou.jpg/250px-Simpson_Disembarkation_of_the_expedition_to_Kertch_at_Kamish_Bournou.jpg)

Disembarkation of the expedition to [Kerch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kerch "Kerch")

In early 1855, the allied Anglo-French commanders decided to send an Anglo-French naval squadron into the [Azov Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azov_Sea "Azov Sea") to undermine Russian communications and supplies to besieged [Sevastopol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sevastopol "Sevastopol"). On 12 May 1855, Anglo-French warships entered the [Kerch Strait](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kerch_Strait "Kerch Strait") and destroyed the coast battery of the Kamishevaya Bay. Once through the Kerch Strait, British and French warships struck at every vestige of Russian power along the coast of the Sea of Azov. Except for [Rostov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rostov-on-Don "Rostov-on-Don") and [Azov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azov "Azov"), no town, depot, building or fortification was immune from attack, and Russian naval power ceased to exist almost overnight. This Allied campaign led to a significant reduction in supplies flowing to the besieged Russian troops at Sevastopol.

On 21 May 1855, the [gunboats](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gunboat "Gunboat") and armed steamers attacked the seaport of [Taganrog](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taganrog "Taganrog"), the most important hub near [Rostov-on-Don](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rostov-on-Don "Rostov-on-Don"). The vast amounts of food, especially bread, wheat, barley and rye, that were amassed in the city after the outbreak of war were prevented from being exported.

The [Governor of Taganrog](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Governor_of_Taganrog "Governor of Taganrog"), [Yegor Tolstoy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yegor_Tolstoy "Yegor Tolstoy"), and Lieutenant-General [Ivan Krasnov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ivan_Krasnov "Ivan Krasnov") refused an allied ultimatum by responding, "Russians never surrender their cities". The Anglo-French squadron bombarded Taganrog for 61⁄2 hours and landed 300 troops near the [Old Stairway](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Depaldo_Stairs "Depaldo Stairs") in the centre of Taganrog, but they were thrown back by [Don Cossacks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Don_Cossacks "Don Cossacks") and a volunteer corps.

In July 1855, the allied squadron tried to go past Taganrog to [Rostov-on-Don](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rostov-on-Don "Rostov-on-Don") by entering the [River Don](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Don_\(river\) "Don (river)") through the [Mius River](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mius_River "Mius River"). On 12 July 1855 HMS *Jasper* grounded near Taganrog thanks to a fisherman who moved [buoys](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buoy "Buoy") into shallow water. The [Cossacks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cossacks "Cossacks") captured the gunboat with all of its guns and blew it up. The third siege attempt was made 19–31 August 1855, but the city was already fortified, and the squadron could not approach close enough for landing operations. The allied fleet left the [Gulf of Taganrog](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taganrog_Bay "Taganrog Bay") on 2 September 1855, with [minor military operations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HMS_Grinder_\(1855\) "HMS Grinder (1855)") along the Azov Sea coast continuing until late 1855.

### Caucasus theatre

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Crimean_War&action=edit&section=17 "Edit section: Caucasus theatre")\]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/The_Armenian_Front_During_the_Crimean_War%2C_1853-56.gif/500px-The_Armenian_Front_During_the_Crimean_War%2C_1853-56.gif)

Caucasus front during the Crimean War

As in the [previous wars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russo-Turkish_Wars "Russo-Turkish Wars"), the Caucasus front was secondary to what happened in the west. Perhaps because of better communications, western events sometimes influenced the east. The main events were the [second capture of Kars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siege_of_Kars "Siege of Kars") and a landing on the [Georgian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georgia_\(country\) "Georgia (country)") coast. Several commanders on both sides were either incompetent or unlucky, and few fought aggressively.<sup id="cite_ref-132" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-132"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>128<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

**1853:** There were four main events. 1. In the north, the Ottomans captured the border fort of Saint Nicholas in a surprise night attack (27/28 October). They then pushed about 20,000 troops across the [Choloki](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Choloki "Choloki") river border. Being outnumbered, the Russians abandoned [Poti](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poti "Poti") and [Redoubt Kali](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Redoubt_Kali "Redoubt Kali") and drew back to [Marani](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marani,_Georgia "Marani, Georgia"). Both sides remained immobile for the next seven months. 2. In the centre the Ottomans moved north from [Ardahan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ardahan "Ardahan") to within cannon-shot of [Akhaltsike](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akhaltsike "Akhaltsike") and awaited reinforcements (13 November), but the Russians routed them. The claimed losses were 4,000 Turks and 400 Russians. 3. In the south about 30,000 Turks slowly moved east to the main Russian concentration at [Gyumri](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gyumri "Gyumri") or Alexandropol (November). They crossed the border and set up artillery south of town. Prince [Vakhtang Orbeliani](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vakhtang_Orbeliani "Vakhtang Orbeliani") tried to drive them off and found himself trapped. The Ottomans failed to press their advantage; the remaining Russians rescued Orbeliani and the Ottomans retired west. Orbeliani lost about 1,000 men from 5,000. The Russians now decided to advance. The Ottomans took up a strong position on the [Kars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kars "Kars") road and attacked-only to be defeated in the [Battle of Başgedikler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Ba%C5%9Fgedikler "Battle of Başgedikler"), losing 6,000 men, half their artillery and all of their supply train. The Russians lost 1,300, including Prince Orbeliani. This was Prince Ellico Orbeliani, whose wife was later kidnapped by [Imam Shamil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imam_Shamil "Imam Shamil") at [Tsinandali](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tsinandali "Tsinandali"). 4. At sea the Turks sent a fleet east, which was destroyed by Admiral Nakhimov at Sinope.

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Kuruk-Dara1.jpg/250px-Kuruk-Dara1.jpg)

General [Bebutashvili](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vasili_Bebutov "Vasili Bebutov") defeated the Ottomans at the [Battle of Kurekdere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Kurekdere "Battle of Kurekdere").

**1854:** The British and French declared war on 28 March.<sup id="cite_ref-Britannica_13-3" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-Britannica-13"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Early in the year on 3 January, the Anglo-French fleet appeared in the Black Sea,<sup id="cite_ref-Britannica_13-4" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-Britannica-13"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> and the Russians abandoned the Black Sea Defensive Line from [Anapa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anapa "Anapa") south. [Nikolay Muravyov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nikolay_Muravyov-Karsky "Nikolay Muravyov-Karsky"), who replaced [Vorontsov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mikhail_Semyonovich_Vorontsov "Mikhail Semyonovich Vorontsov"), fearing an Anglo-French landing in conjunction with [Shamil, 3rd Imam of Dagestan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shamil,_3rd_Imam_of_Dagestan "Shamil, 3rd Imam of Dagestan") and the [Persians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qajar_Iran "Qajar Iran"), recommended withdrawal north of the Caucasus. For that purpose, he was replaced by [Aleksandr Baryatinsky](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aleksandr_Baryatinsky "Aleksandr Baryatinsky"). When the allies chose a land attack on Sevastopol, any plan for a landing in the east was abandoned.

In the north, [Georgiy Evseevich Eristov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georgiy_Evseevich_Eristov "Georgiy Evseevich Eristov") pushed southwest, fought two battles, forced the Ottomans back to [Batumi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Batumi "Batumi"), retired behind the Cholok river and suspended action for the rest of the year (June). In the far south, Wrangel pushed west, fought a battle and occupied [Bayazit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Do%C4%9Fubayaz%C4%B1t "Doğubayazıt"). In the centre. the main forces stood at Kars and Gyumri. Both slowly approached along the Kars-Gyumri road and faced each other, neither side choosing to fight (June–July). On 4 August, Russian scouts saw a movement which they thought was the start of a withdrawal, the Russians advanced and the Ottomans attacked first. [They were defeated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Kurekdere "Battle of Kurekdere") and lost 8,000 men to the Russian 3,000. Also, 10,000 irregulars deserted to their villages. Both sides withdrew to their former positions. About then, the [Persians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qajar_Iran "Qajar Iran") made a semi-secret agreement to remain neutral in exchange for the cancellation of the indemnity from the previous war.

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Thomas_Jones_Barker_The_Capitulation_of_Kars_1855.jpg/250px-Thomas_Jones_Barker_The_Capitulation_of_Kars_1855.jpg)

The Capitulation of Kars

**1855: Siege of Kars:** Up to May 1855, Ottomans forces in the east were reduced from 120,000 to 75,000, mostly by disease. The local [Armenian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenia "Armenia") population kept Muravyov well-informed about the Ottomans at Kars and he judged they had about five months of supplies. He therefore decided to control the surrounding area with cavalry and starve them out. He started in May and by June was south and west of the town. A relieving force fell back and there was a possibility of taking [Erzurum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erzurum "Erzurum"), but Muravyov chose not to. In late September he learned of the fall of Sevastopol and a Turkish landing at Batum. This led him to reverse policy and try a direct attack. It failed, the Russians losing 8,000 men and the Turks 1,500 (29 September). The blockade continued and Kars surrendered on 28 November.

**1855: Georgian coast:** Omar Pasha, the Turkish commander at Crimea had long wanted to land in Georgia, but the western powers vetoed it. When they relented in August most of the campaigning season was lost. In 8 September Turks landed at Batum, but the main concentration was at [Sukhum Kale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sukhumi "Sukhumi"). This required a 100-mile march south through a country with poor roads. In essence, it was a military demonstration in order to frighten the Russian command and force it to lift the siege of the fortress of Kars. "All luck depended on whether Muravyov (the Russian commander) would be scared or not".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETarle1950493_133-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTETarle1950493-133"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>129<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> But the Russian command did not see a serious threat, the Siege of Kars was continued. The Russians planned to hold the line of the [Ingur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enguri "Enguri") river which separates [Abkhazia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abkhazia "Abkhazia") from Georgia proper. Omar crossed the Ingur on 7 November and then wasted a great deal of time, the Russians doing little. By 2 December he had reached the [Tskhenistsqali](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tskhenistsqali "Tskhenistsqali"), the rainy season had started, his camps were submerged in mud and there was no bread. Learning of the fall of Kars he withdrew to the Ingur. The Russians did nothing and he evacuated to Batum in February of the following year.

### Baltic theatre

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Crimean_War&action=edit&section=18 "Edit section: Baltic theatre")\]

See also: [Charles John Napier § Baltic Campaign](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_John_Napier#Baltic_Campaign "Charles John Napier"), and [Åland War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%85land_War "Åland War")

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Escadre_franco-anglaise_devant_Bomarsund.jpg/250px-Escadre_franco-anglaise_devant_Bomarsund.jpg)

[Bombardment of Bomarsund](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Bomarsund "Battle of Bomarsund") during the Crimean War, by [Antoine Léon Morel-Fatio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antoine_L%C3%A9on_Morel-Fatio "Antoine Léon Morel-Fatio")

The [Baltic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baltic_Sea "Baltic Sea") was a forgotten theatre of the Crimean War.<sup id="cite_ref-134" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-134"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>130<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Popularisation of events elsewhere overshadowed the significance of this theatre, which was close to Saint Petersburg, the Russian capital. In April 1854, an Anglo-French fleet entered the Baltic to attack the Russian naval base of [Kronstadt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kronstadt "Kronstadt") and the [Russian fleet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baltic_Fleet "Baltic Fleet") that was stationed there.<sup id="cite_ref-Colvile1_135-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-Colvile1-135"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>131<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> In August 1854, the combined British and French fleet returned to Kronstadt for another attempt. The outnumbered Russian Baltic Fleet confined its movements to the areas around its fortifications. At the same time, the British and French commanders Sir [Charles Napier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_Napier_\(Royal_Navy_officer\) "Charles Napier (Royal Navy officer)") and [Alexandre Ferdinand Parseval-Deschenes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexandre_Ferdinand_Parseval-Deschenes "Alexandre Ferdinand Parseval-Deschenes") although they led the largest fleet assembled since the [Napoleonic Wars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Napoleonic_Wars "Napoleonic Wars"), considered the [Sveaborg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suomenlinna "Suomenlinna") fortress too well-defended to engage. Thus, shelling of the Russian batteries was limited to two attempts in 1854 and 1855, and initially, the attacking fleets limited their actions to blockading Russian trade in the [Gulf of Finland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Finland "Gulf of Finland").<sup id="cite_ref-Colvile1_135-1" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-Colvile1-135"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>131<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Naval attacks on other ports, such as the ones in the island of [Hogland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gogland "Gogland") in the Gulf of Finland, proved more successful. Additionally, allies conducted raids on less fortified sections of the [Finnish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Finland "Grand Duchy of Finland") coast.<sup id="cite_ref-136" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-136"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>132<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> These battles are known in [Finland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Finland "Finland") as the [Åland War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%85land_War "Åland War").

Russia depended on imports—both for its domestic economy and for the supply of its military forces: the blockade forced Russia to rely on more expensive overland shipments from Prussia. The blockade seriously undermined the Russian export economy and helped shorten the war.<sup id="cite_ref-137" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-137"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>133<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

The burning of tar warehouses and ships led to international criticism, and in London the MP [Thomas Milner Gibson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Milner_Gibson "Thomas Milner Gibson") demanded in the House of Commons that the [First Lord of the Admiralty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Lord_of_the_Admiralty "First Lord of the Admiralty") explain "a system which carried on a great war by plundering and destroying the property of defenceless villagers".<sup id="cite_ref-138" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-138"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>134<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> In fact, the operations in the Baltic sea were in the nature of binding forces. It was very important to divert Russian forces from the south or, more precisely, not to allow Nicholas to transfer to Crimea a huge army guarding the Baltic coast and the capital.<sup id="cite_ref-139" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-139"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>135<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> This goal Anglo-French forces achieved. The Russian Army in Crimea was forced to act without superiority in forces.

In August 1854 a Franco-British naval force captured and destroyed the [Russian Bomarsund fortress](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Bomarsund "Battle of Bomarsund") on [Åland Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%85land "Åland"). In the August 1855, the Western Allied Baltic Fleet [tried to destroy heavily defended Russian dockyards at Sveaborg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bombardment_of_Sveaborg "Bombardment of Sveaborg") outside [Helsinki](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Helsinki "Helsinki"). More than 1,000 enemy guns tested the strength of the fortress for two days. Despite the shelling, the sailors of the 120-gun ship *[Rossiya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_ship_of_the_line_Rossiya "Russian ship of the line Rossiya")*, led by Captain Viktor Poplonsky, defended the entrance to the harbour. The Allies fired over 20,000 shells but failed to defeat the Russian batteries. The British then built a massive new fleet of more than 350 gunboats and mortar vessels,<sup id="cite_ref-140" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-140"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>136<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> which was known as the [Great Armament](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Armament "Great Armament"), but the war ended before the attack was launched.

Part of the Russian resistance was credited to the deployment of newly invented [naval mines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naval_mine "Naval mine"). Perhaps the most influential contributor to the development of naval mining was a Swede resident in Russia, the inventor and civil engineer [Immanuel Nobel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Immanuel_Nobel "Immanuel Nobel") (the father of [Alfred Nobel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alfred_Nobel "Alfred Nobel")). Immanuel Nobel helped the Russian war effort by applying his knowledge of industrial explosives, such as [nitroglycerin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitroglycerin "Nitroglycerin") and [gunpowder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gunpowder "Gunpowder"). An account given in 1860 by [United States Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Army "United States Army") Major [Richard Delafield](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Delafield "Richard Delafield") dates modern naval mining to the Crimean War: "[Torpedo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Torpedo "Torpedo") mines, if I may use this name given by [Fulton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Fulton "Robert Fulton") to 'self-acting mines underwater', were among the novelties attempted by the Russians in their defences about Cronstadt and Sevastopol."<sup id="cite_ref-mines_141-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-mines-141"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>137<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

For the campaign of 1856, Britain and France planned an attack on the main base of the Russian Navy in the Baltic sea—Kronstadt. The attack was to be carried out using armoured floating batteries. The use of the latter proved to be highly effective in the attack on Kinburn on the Black Sea in 1855. Undoubtedly, this threat contributed on the part of Russia the decision on the conclusion of peace on unfavourable terms.

### Pacific theatre

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Crimean_War&action=edit&section=20 "Edit section: Pacific theatre")\]

Main article: [Siege of Petropavlovsk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siege_of_Petropavlovsk "Siege of Petropavlovsk")

Minor naval skirmishes also occurred in the Far East, where at [Petropavlovsk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Petropavlovsk-Kamchatsky "Petropavlovsk-Kamchatsky") on the [Kamchatka Peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kamchatka_Peninsula "Kamchatka Peninsula") a British and French Allied squadron including [HMS *Pique*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HMS_Pique_\(1834\) "HMS Pique (1834)") under Rear Admiral [David Price](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Price_\(Royal_Navy_officer\) "David Price (Royal Navy officer)") and a French force under Counter-Admiral [Auguste Febvrier Despointes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Auguste_Febvrier_Despointes "Auguste Febvrier Despointes") [besieged](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siege_of_Petropavlovsk "Siege of Petropavlovsk") a smaller [Russian force](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Fleet_\(Russia\) "Pacific Fleet (Russia)") under Rear Admiral [Yevfimiy Putyatin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yevfimiy_Putyatin "Yevfimiy Putyatin"). In September 1854, an Allied landing force was beaten back with heavy casualties, and the Allies withdrew. The victory at Petropavlovsk was for Russia in the words of the future [Minister of War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ministry_of_War_of_the_Russian_Empire "Ministry of War of the Russian Empire") [Dmitry Milyutin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dmitry_Milyutin "Dmitry Milyutin") "a ray of light among the dark clouds". The Russians escaped under the cover of snow in early 1855 after Allied reinforcements arrived in the region.

The Anglo-French forces in the [Far East](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Far_East "Far East") also made several small landings on [Sakhalin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sakhalin "Sakhalin") and [Urup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Urup "Urup"), one of the [Kuril Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kuril_Islands "Kuril Islands").<sup id="cite_ref-143" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-143"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>139<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

### Piedmontese involvement

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Crimean_War&action=edit&section=21 "Edit section: Piedmontese involvement")\]

Main article: [Crimean War order of battle: Kingdom of Sardinia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crimean_War_order_of_battle:_Kingdom_of_Sardinia "Crimean War order of battle: Kingdom of Sardinia")

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Crimea_Cernaia_DeStefani.JPG/250px-Crimea_Cernaia_DeStefani.JPG)

The Italian [Bersaglieri](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bersaglieri "Bersaglieri") halt the Russian attack during the [Battle of the Chernaya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_the_Chernaya "Battle of the Chernaya").

[Camillo di Cavour](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Camillo_Benso,_Count_of_Cavour "Camillo Benso, Count of Cavour"), under orders of [Victor Emmanuel II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Victor_Emmanuel_II "Victor Emmanuel II") of [Piedmont-Sardinia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Sardinia_\(1720%E2%80%931861\) "Kingdom of Sardinia (1720–1861)"), sent an expeditionary corps of 15,000 soldiers, commanded by General [Alfonso La Marmora](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alfonso_La_Marmora "Alfonso La Marmora"), to side with French and British forces during the war.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEArnold2002[httpsbooksgooglecombooksid_UreS--MoD0CpgPA112_111–112_144-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEArnold2002[httpsbooksgooglecombooksid_UreS--MoD0CpgPA112_111%E2%80%93112-144"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>140<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> This was an attempt at gaining the favour of the French, especially when the issue of uniting Italy would become an important matter. The deployment of Italian troops to Crimea, and the gallantry shown by them in the Battle of the Chernaya (16 August 1855) and in the Siege of Sevastopol, allowed the Kingdom of Sardinia to be among the participants at the peace conference at the end of the war, where it could address the issue of the *[Risorgimento](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Risorgimento "Risorgimento")* to other European powers.

### Greece

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Crimean_War&action=edit&section=22 "Edit section: Greece")\]

Main article: [Greek Volunteer Legion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_Volunteer_Legion "Greek Volunteer Legion")

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Greek_volunteers_in_Sevastopol_1854.jpg/250px-Greek_volunteers_in_Sevastopol_1854.jpg)

A [Greek legion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_Volunteer_Legion "Greek Volunteer Legion") fought for Russia at Sevastopol

Greece played a peripheral role in the war. When Russia attacked the Ottoman Empire in 1853, King [Otto of Greece](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Otto_of_Greece "Otto of Greece") saw an opportunity to expand north and south into Ottoman areas that had large Greek Christian majorities. Greece did not coordinate its plans with Russia, did not declare war, and received no outside military or financial support. Greece, an Orthodox nation, had considerable support in Russia, but the Russian government decided it was too dangerous to help Greece expand its holdings.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges201032–40_145-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges201032%E2%80%9340-145"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>141<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> When the Russians invaded the Principalities, the Ottoman forces were tied down so Greece invaded [Thessaly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thessaly "Thessaly") and [Epirus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epirus "Epirus"). To block further Greek moves, the British and French occupied the main Greek port at [Piraeus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Piraeus "Piraeus") from April 1854 to February 1857,<sup id="cite_ref-146" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-146"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>142<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> and effectively neutralized the [Greek Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hellenic_Army "Hellenic Army"). The Greeks, gambling on a Russian victory, incited the large-scale [Epirus Revolt of 1854](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epirus_Revolt_of_1854 "Epirus Revolt of 1854") as well as uprisings in [Ottoman Crete](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Crete "Ottoman Crete"). The insurrections were failures that were easily crushed by the Ottomans' allied [Egyptian Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egyptian_Army#Under_the_Muhammad_Ali_Dynasty "Egyptian Army"). Greece was not invited to the peace conference and made no gains out of the war.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010139_147-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010139-147"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>143<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBadem2010183_148-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBadem2010183-148"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>144<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The frustrated Greek leadership blamed the King for failing to take advantage of the situation; his popularity plunged and he was forced to abdicate in 1862.

In addition, a 1,000-strong [Greek Volunteer Legion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_Volunteer_Legion "Greek Volunteer Legion") was formed in the Danubian Principalities in 1854 and later fought at Sevastopol.<sup id="cite_ref-149" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-149"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>145<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

## End of the war

### British position

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Crimean_War&action=edit&section=25 "Edit section: British position")\]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/Arundel_Russian_Bell_6.JPG/250px-Arundel_Russian_Bell_6.JPG)

One of three 17th-century church bells in [Arundel Castle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arundel_Castle "Arundel Castle"), England, which were taken from [Sevastopol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sevastopol "Sevastopol") as trophies at the end of the Crimean War

Dissatisfaction with the conduct of the war was growing with the public in Britain and other countries and was worsened by reports of fiascos, especially the devastating losses of the Charge of the Light Brigade at the Battle of Balaclava. On Sunday, 21 January 1855, a "snowball riot" occurred in [Trafalgar Square](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trafalgar_Square "Trafalgar Square") near [St Martin-in-the-Fields](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/St_Martin-in-the-Fields "St Martin-in-the-Fields") in which 1,500 people gathered to [protest against the war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anti-war_movement "Anti-war movement") by pelting cabs and pedestrians with snowballs.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated599_151-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-autogenerated599-151"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>147<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> When the police intervened, the snowballs were directed at the constables. The riot was finally put down by troops and police acting with truncheons.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated599_151-1" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-autogenerated599-151"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>147<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> In [Parliament](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parliament_of_the_United_Kingdom "Parliament of the United Kingdom"), the Conservatives demanded an accounting of all soldiers, cavalry and sailors sent to Crimea and accurate figures as to the number of casualties sustained by all British armed forces in Crimea, especially concerning the Battle of Balaclava. When Parliament passed a bill to investigate by the vote of 305 to 148, Aberdeen said he had lost a vote of no confidence and resigned as prime minister on 30 January 1855.<sup id="cite_ref-152" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-152"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>148<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The veteran former Foreign Secretary [Lord Palmerston](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lord_Palmerston "Lord Palmerston") became prime minister.<sup id="cite_ref-153" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-153"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>149<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Palmerston took a hard line and wanted to expand the war, foment unrest inside the Russian Empire and reduce the Russian threat to Europe permanently. [Sweden–Norway](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Union_between_Sweden_and_Norway "Union between Sweden and Norway") and Prussia were willing to join Britain and France, and Russia was isolated.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010400–402,_406–408_154-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010400%E2%80%93402,_406%E2%80%93408-154"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>150<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

### Peace negotiations

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Crimean_War&action=edit&section=26 "Edit section: Peace negotiations")\]

France, which had sent far more soldiers to the war and suffered far more casualties than Britain had, wanted the war to end, as did Austria.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010402–405_155-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010402%E2%80%93405-155"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>151<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

Negotiations began in Paris in February 1856 and were surprisingly easy. France, under the leadership of Napoleon III, had no special interests in the Black Sea and so did not support the harsh British and Austrian proposals.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETarle1950533_156-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTETarle1950533-156"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>152<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

Peace negotiations at the [Congress of Paris](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Congress_of_Paris_\(1856\) "Congress of Paris (1856)") resulted in the signing of the [Treaty of Paris](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_\(1856\) "Treaty of Paris (1856)") on 30 March 1856.<sup id="cite_ref-157" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-157"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>153<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> In compliance with Article III, Russia restored to the Ottoman Empire the city and the citadel of Kars and "all other parts of the Ottoman territory of which the Russian troop were in possession". Russia returned the [Southern Bessarabia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_Bessarabia "Southern Bessarabia") to Moldavia.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTESmall2007188–190_158-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTESmall2007188%E2%80%93190-158"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>154<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Baumgart_159-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-Baumgart-159"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>155<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> By Article IV, Britain, France, Sardinia and Ottoman Empire restored to Russia "the towns and ports of Sevastopol, Balaklava, Kamish, Eupatoria, Kerch, [Jenikale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yeni-Kale "Yeni-Kale"), [Kinburn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kinburn_Spit "Kinburn Spit") as well as all other territories occupied by the allied troops". In conformity with Articles XI and XIII, the Tsar and the Sultan agreed not to establish any naval or military arsenal on the Black Sea coast. The Black Sea clauses weakened Russia, which no longer posed a naval threat to the Ottomans. The Principalities of Moldavia and Wallachia were nominally returned to the Ottoman Empire, and the Austrian Empire was forced to abandon its annexation and to end its occupation of them,<sup id="cite_ref-160" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-160"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>156<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> but they in practice became independent. The Treaty of Paris admitted the Ottoman Empire to the Concert of Europe, and the great powers pledged to respect its independence and territorial integrity.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010432–433_161-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010432%E2%80%93433-161"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>157<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

### Aftermath in Russia

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Crimean_War&action=edit&section=27 "Edit section: Aftermath in Russia")\]

Some members of the Russian intelligentsia saw defeat as a pressure to modernise their society. [Grand Duke Constantine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grand_Duke_Konstantin_Nikolayevich_of_Russia "Grand Duke Konstantin Nikolayevich of Russia"), a son of the Tsar, remarked:<sup id="cite_ref-162" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-162"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>158<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

> We cannot deceive ourselves any longer; we must say that we are both weaker and poorer than the first-class powers, and furthermore poorer not only in material terms but in mental resources, especially in matters of administration.

## Long-term effects

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Crimean_War&action=edit&section=28 "Edit section: Long-term effects")\]

Main article: [Treaty of Paris (1856)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_\(1856\) "Treaty of Paris (1856)")

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Edouard_Dubufe_Congr%C3%A8s_de_Paris.jpg/220px-Edouard_Dubufe_Congr%C3%A8s_de_Paris.jpg)

*[The Congress of Paris](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Congress_of_Paris "The Congress of Paris")* by [Edouard Dubufe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edouard_Dubufe "Edouard Dubufe"). The [Treaty of Paris](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_\(1856\) "Treaty of Paris (1856)") brought an end to the war.

[Orlando Figes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orlando_Figes "Orlando Figes") points to the long-term damage Russia suffered: "The demilitarization of the Black Sea was a major blow to Russia, which was no longer able to protect its vulnerable southern coastal frontier against the British or any other fleet... The destruction of the Russian Black Sea Fleet, Sevastopol and other naval docks was a humiliation. No compulsory disarmament had ever been imposed on a great power previously... The Allies did not really think that they were dealing with a European power in Russia. They regarded Russia as a semi-Asiatic state... In Russia itself, the Crimean defeat discredited the armed services and highlighted the need to modernize the country's defences, not just in the strictly military sense, but also through the building of railways, industrialization, sound finances and so on... The image many Russians had built up of their country—the biggest, richest and most powerful in the world—had suddenly been shattered. Russia's backwardness had been exposed... The Crimean disaster had exposed the shortcomings of every institution in Russia—not just the corruption and incompetence of the military command, the technological backwardness of the army and navy, or the inadequate roads and lack of railways that accounted for the chronic problems of supply, but the poor condition and illiteracy of the serfs who made up the armed forces, the inability of the serf economy to sustain a state of war against industrial powers, and the failures of autocracy itself."<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010442–443_163-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010442%E2%80%93443-163"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>159<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e5/Balance1855.jpg/250px-Balance1855.jpg)

*The New European Balance in Europe*, Russian propaganda poster c. 1855

Surprisingly, after the war, the Russian-French rapprochement came, which corresponded to the plans of both sides. France persuaded the Russians to support their claims to return to the territories before the [Congress of Vienna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Congress_of_Vienna "Congress of Vienna"), in exchange for ceasing to act as a guarantor of the Paris peace. [Alexander II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander_II_of_Russia "Alexander II of Russia") stated in response to his minister's remark: "It would undoubtedly be more useful for our interests to have him among our allies if we could rely on him."<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEAirapetov2017199_164-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAirapetov2017199-164"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>160<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/CrimeaWarMemorialLondon.jpg/250px-CrimeaWarMemorialLondon.jpg)

[Crimean War Memorial](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crimean_War_Memorial "Crimean War Memorial") at Waterloo Place, [St James's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/St_James%27s "St James's"), London

The Treaty of Paris stood until 1871, when Prussia defeated France in the [Franco-Prussian War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franco-Prussian_War "Franco-Prussian War") of 1870–71. While Prussia and several other German states united to form a powerful [German Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_Empire "German Empire") in January 1871, the French deposed Emperor Napoleon III and proclaimed the [French Third Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Third_Republic "French Third Republic") (September 1870). During his reign, Napoleon, eager for the support of the United Kingdom, had opposed Russia over the Eastern Question. Russian interference in the Ottoman Empire did not in any significant manner threaten the interests of France ([Kissinger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kissinger "Kissinger") uses Napoleon's pandering to journalists and public opinion on this subject—at the expense of the true interests of France—as an example of [strategic frivolity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strategic_frivolity "Strategic frivolity")<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEKissinger2012_165-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEKissinger2012-165"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>161<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>), and France abandoned its opposition to Russia after the establishment of the republic. Encouraged by the new attitude of French diplomacy after the surrenders of the besieged French Army at [Sedan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Sedan "Battle of Sedan") and later [Metz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siege_of_Metz_\(1870\) "Siege of Metz (1870)") and supported by the German Chancellor [Otto von Bismarck](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Otto_von_Bismarck "Otto von Bismarck"), Russia in October 1870 renounced the Black Sea clauses of the treaty agreed to in 1856. As the United Kingdom with [Austria-Hungary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austria-Hungary "Austria-Hungary")<sup id="cite_ref-166" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-166"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>162<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> could not enforce the clauses, Russia once again established a fleet in the Black Sea.

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Welsford-Parker_Monument_at_the_entrance_to_the_Old_Burying_Ground_in_Halifax%2C_Nova_Scotia%2C_Canada.jpg/250px-Welsford-Parker_Monument_at_the_entrance_to_the_Old_Burying_Ground_in_Halifax%2C_Nova_Scotia%2C_Canada.jpg)

[Sebastopol Monument](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sebastopol_Monument "Sebastopol Monument"), [Halifax, Nova Scotia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Halifax_Regional_Municipality "Halifax Regional Municipality") – the only Crimean War Monument in North America

After being defeated in the Crimean War, Russia feared that [Russian Alaska](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_Alaska "Russian Alaska") would be easily captured in any future war with the British; therefore, Alexander II opted to [sell the territory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alaska_Purchase "Alaska Purchase") to the [United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States "United States").<sup id="cite_ref-167" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-167"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>163<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

A [Greek tortoise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_tortoise "Greek tortoise") named [Timothy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timothy_\(tortoise\) "Timothy (tortoise)") was found on a Portuguese ship by Captain John Guy Courtenay-Everard on [HMS *Queen*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HMS_Queen_\(1839\) "HMS Queen (1839)") in 1854. Serving as a [mascot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mascot "Mascot") throughout the war, when she died in 2004 this made her the last living [veteran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Veteran "Veteran") of the Crimean war.<sup id="cite_ref-168" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-168"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>164<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

Historian Norman Rich argues that the war was not an accident, but was sought out by the determination of the British and French not to allow Russia an honourable retreat. Both insisted on a military victory to enhance their prestige in European affairs when a non-violent peaceful political solution was available. The war then wrecked the Concert of Europe, which had long kept the peace.<sup id="cite_ref-169" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-169"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>165<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

Turkish historian Candan Badem wrote, "Victory in this war did not bring any significant material gain, not even a war indemnity. On the other hand, the Ottoman treasury was nearly bankrupted due to war expenses". Badem adds that the Ottomans achieved no significant territorial gains, lost the right to a navy in the Black Sea, and failed to gain status as a great power. Further, the war gave impetus to the [union of the Danubian principalities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Principalities_of_Moldavia_and_Wallachia "United Principalities of Moldavia and Wallachia") and ultimately to their independence.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEBadem2010403_170-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEBadem2010403-170"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>166<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/%D0%9F%D0%B0%D0%BC%27%D1%8F%D1%82%D0%BD%D0%B8%D0%BA_%D0%95._%D0%86._%D0%A2%D0%BE%D1%82%D0%BB%D0%B5%D0%B1%D0%B5%D0%BD%D1%83_5.jpg/250px-%D0%9F%D0%B0%D0%BC%27%D1%8F%D1%82%D0%BD%D0%B8%D0%BA_%D0%95._%D0%86._%D0%A2%D0%BE%D1%82%D0%BB%D0%B5%D0%B1%D0%B5%D0%BD%D1%83_5.jpg)

Monument to the [Baltic German](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baltic_Germans "Baltic Germans") military engineer and general of the Imperial Russian Army [Eduard Totleben](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eduard_Totleben "Eduard Totleben") in Sevastopol, Crimea

The treaty punished the defeated Russia, but in the long run, Austria lost the most from the war despite having barely taken part in it.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010433_171-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010433-171"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>167<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Having abandoned its alliance with Russia, Austria remained diplomatically isolated following the war,<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010433_171-1" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010433-171"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>167<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> which contributed to its disastrous defeats in the 1859 [Franco-Austrian War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franco-Austrian_War "Franco-Austrian War") that resulted in the cession of [Lombardy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lombardy "Lombardy") to the [Kingdom of Sardinia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Sardinia_\(1720%E2%80%931861\) "Kingdom of Sardinia (1720–1861)") and later in the loss of the Habsburg rule of [Tuscany](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grand_Duchy_of_Tuscany "Grand Duchy of Tuscany") and [Modena](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duchy_of_Modena_and_Reggio "Duchy of Modena and Reggio"), which meant the end of Austrian influence in peninsular Italy. Furthermore, Russia did not do anything to assist its former ally, Austria, in the 1866 [Austro-Prussian War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austro-Prussian_War "Austro-Prussian War"),<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010433_171-2" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010433-171"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>167<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> when Austria lost [Venetia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Lombardy%E2%80%93Venetia "Kingdom of Lombardy–Venetia") and, more importantly, its influence in most German-speaking lands. The status of Austria as a great power, with the unifications of [Germany](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_unification "German unification") and [Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_unification "Italian unification"), now became very precarious. It had to [compromise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ausgleich "Ausgleich") with [Hungary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hungary "Kingdom of Hungary"); the two countries shared the Danubian Empire. With France now hostile to Germany and gravitating towards Russia, and with Russia competing with the newly renamed [Austro-Hungarian Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austro-Hungarian_Empire "Austro-Hungarian Empire") for an increased role in the Balkans at the expense of the Ottoman Empire, the foundations were in place for building the diplomatic alliances that would shape the [First World War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_World_War "First World War").

The Treaty's guarantees to preserve Ottoman territories were broken 21 years later when Russia, exploiting nationalist unrest in the Balkans and seeking to regain lost prestige, once again declared war on the Ottoman Empire on 24 April 1877. In this later [Russo-Turkish War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russo-Turkish_War_\(1877%E2%80%931878\) "Russo-Turkish War (1877–1878)") the states of [Romania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romania "Romania"), [Serbia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Principality_of_Serbia "Principality of Serbia"), and [Montenegro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Principality_of_Montenegro "Principality of Montenegro") gained international recognition of their independence and [Bulgaria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Principality_of_Bulgaria "Principality of Bulgaria") achieved its autonomy from direct Ottoman rule. Russia took over Southern Bessarabia,<sup id="cite_ref-172" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-172"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>168<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> lost in 1856. The regions of Batum and Kars, as well as those inhabited by [Adjarians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adjarians "Adjarians") (Muslim [Georgians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georgians "Georgians")) and [Armenians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenians "Armenians"), were also annexed to Russia in the Caucasus. At the same time, "protectors" of the Ottoman Empire Britain received [Cyprus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyprus "Cyprus") as a colonial possession, while Austria-Hungary [occupied](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austro-Hungarian_rule_in_Bosnia_and_Herzegovina "Austro-Hungarian rule in Bosnia and Herzegovina") and [annexed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bosnian_Crisis "Bosnian Crisis") [Bosnia and Herzegovina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina "Bosnia and Herzegovina") in 1908. Finally, Ottoman rule in the Balkans ended after the [First Balkan War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Balkan_War "First Balkan War") of 1912, when the combined forces of the Balkan states defeated it.

The Crimean War marked the re-ascendancy of France to the position of pre-eminent power on the Continent,<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010411_173-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010411-173"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>169<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> the continued decline of the Ottoman Empire and a period of crisis for Imperial Russia. As Fuller notes, "Russia had been beaten on the Crimean Peninsula, and the military feared that it would inevitably be beaten again unless steps were taken to surmount its military weakness."<sup id="cite_ref-174" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-174"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>170<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> To compensate for its defeat in the Crimean War, the Russian Empire then embarked in more intensive expansion in Asia, partially to restore national pride and partially to distract Britain on the world stage, intensifying the [Great Game](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Game "Great Game").<sup id="cite_ref-:22_175-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/:22-175"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>171<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:04_176-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/:04-176"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>172<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

The war also marked the demise of the first phase of the [Concert of Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Concert_of_Europe "Concert of Europe"), the balance-of-power system that had dominated Europe since the Congress of Vienna in 1815 and had included France, Russia, Prussia, Austria and the United Kingdom. From 1854 to 1871, the Concert of Europe concept was weakened, leading to the crises that were the [unifications of Germany](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unification_of_Germany "Unification of Germany") and of [Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unification_of_Italy "Unification of Italy"), before a resurgence of great power conferences.<sup id="cite_ref-177" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-177"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>173<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

In 1870, Prussia persuaded Russia to remain neutral in the Franco-Prussian war.<sup id="cite_ref-178" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-178"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>174<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Bismarck, having declared it impossible to keep 100 million Russians in a humiliated position without sovereign rights to their Black Sea coastline,<sup id="cite_ref-179" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-179"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>175<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> supported Russia against the Treaty of Paris, and in return, Prussia achieved freedom of action against France in 1870–71 and inflicted a crushing defeat on it.

An envoy from [India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Raj "British Raj") who visited the war front after arriving to [Constantinople](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Istanbul "Istanbul") from a failed mission in [England](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/England "England"), [Azimullah Khan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azimullah_Khan "Azimullah Khan") observed exhaustion and fatigue in the British soldiers and set to exploit a perceived weak state of the British Empire upon his return which consequently planted the seeds for the [Indian War of Independence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Rebellion_of_1857 "Indian Rebellion of 1857").<sup id="cite_ref-180" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-180"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>176<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

### Historical analysis

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Crimean_War&action=edit&section=29 "Edit section: Historical analysis")\]

According to historian [Shepard Clough](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shepard_B._Clough "Shepard B. Clough"), the war

> was not the result of a calculated plan, nor even of hasty last-minute decisions made under stress. It was the consequence of more than two years of fatal blundering in slow-motion by inept statesmen who had months to reflect upon the actions they took. It arose from Napoleon's search for prestige; Nicholas's quest for control over the Straits; his naïve miscalculation of the probable reactions of the European powers; the failure of those powers to make their positions clear; and the pressure of public opinion in Britain and Constantinople at crucial moments.<sup id="cite_ref-181" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-181"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>177<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

The view of "diplomatic drift" as the cause of the war was first popularised by [A. W. Kinglake](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A._W._Kinglake "A. W. Kinglake"), who portrayed the British as victims of newspaper sensationalism and duplicitous French and Ottoman diplomacy.

More recently, historians [Andrew Lambert](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andrew_Lambert "Andrew Lambert") and [Winfried Baumgart](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Winfried_Baumgart "Winfried Baumgart") have argued that Britain was following a geopolitical strategy in aiming to destroy the fledgling Russian Navy, which might challenge the Royal Navy for control of the seas, and that the war was also a joint European response to a century of Russian expansion not just southwards but also into Western Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_74-1" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/:0-74"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>70<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Baumgart_159-1" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-Baumgart-159"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>155<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

### Casualties

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Crimean_War&action=edit&section=30 "Edit section: Casualties")\]

The war undoubtedly became one of the bloodiest in the history of Europe at that time, but data on casualties vary greatly. The European states, [Russia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_Empire "Russian Empire"), [France](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_French_Empire "Second French Empire") and [Britain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland "United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland") suffered the greatest losses. Sources agree on the definition of the losses of the last two, these are 95,615 dead on the part of France, 22,182 on the part of Britain and 2,166 dead Italians,<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEDumasVedel-Petersen192341–42_182-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDumasVedel-Petersen192341%E2%80%9342-182"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>178<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEClodfelter2017180_5-9" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEClodfelter2017180-5"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Turkish losses range from 45,400 dead<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEClodfelter2017180_5-10" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEClodfelter2017180-5"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> to 400,000.<sup id="cite_ref-Tashlykov_7-2" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-Tashlykov-7"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Data on losses among Russians also vary greatly. The minimum death toll is 73,125 dead,<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEDumasVedel-Petersen192342_11-1" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDumasVedel-Petersen192342-11"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> rising to 522,000.<sup id="cite_ref-Tashlykov_7-3" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-Tashlykov-7"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> There are also data on 450,015 dead and killed.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEClodfelter2017180_5-11" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEClodfelter2017180-5"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Historian [Yevgeny Tarle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yevgeny_Tarle "Yevgeny Tarle") described the war as a "bloody massacre".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTETarle1959553_183-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTETarle1959553-183"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>179<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

## Documentation

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Crimean_War&action=edit&section=31 "Edit section: Documentation")\]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/Crimean_War_1854-56_Q71114.jpg/250px-Crimean_War_1854-56_Q71114.jpg)

Officers of the [41st Foot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/41st_\(Welch\)_Regiment_of_Foot "41st (Welch) Regiment of Foot") on 5 November 1854, photograph by [James Robertson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Robertson_\(photographer\) "James Robertson (photographer)")

Documentation of the war was provided by William Howard Russell, who wrote for *The Times* newspaper, and by [Roger Fenton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roger_Fenton "Roger Fenton")'s photographs.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010306–309_184-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010306%E2%80%93309-184"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>180<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> News from war correspondents reached all of the nations involved in the war and kept the public citizenry of those nations better informed of the day-to-day events of the war than had been the case in any earlier war. The British public was very well informed on the day-to-day realities of the war. After the French extended the telegraph to the coast of the Black Sea in late 1854, news reached [London](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/London "London") in two days. When the British laid an underwater cable to Crimea in April 1855, news reached London in a few hours. The daily news reports energised public opinion, which brought down the Aberdeen government and carried Lord Palmerston into office as prime minister.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010304–311_185-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010304%E2%80%93311-185"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>181<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-186" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-186"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>182<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

[Leo Tolstoy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leo_Tolstoy "Leo Tolstoy") wrote a few short sketches on the Siege of Sevastopol, collected in *[Sevastopol Sketches](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sevastopol_Sketches "Sevastopol Sketches")*. The stories detail the lives of the Russian soldiers and citizens in Sevastopol during the siege. Because of this work, Tolstoy has been called the world's first [war correspondent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/War_correspondent "War correspondent").<sup id="cite_ref-187" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-187"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>183<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

## Criticisms and reform

\[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Crimean_War&action=edit&section=32 "Edit section: Criticisms and reform")\]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/Florence_Nightingale_three_quarter_length.jpg/170px-Florence_Nightingale_three_quarter_length.jpg)

During the Crimean War, [Florence Nightingale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Florence_Nightingale "Florence Nightingale") and her team of nurses used new statistical approaches to raise awareness, clean up the military hospitals and set up the first training school for nurses in the United Kingdom.<sup id="cite_ref-188" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-188"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>184<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

Historian [R. B. McCallum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/R._B._McCallum "R. B. McCallum") points out the war was enthusiastically supported by the British populace as it was happening, but the mood changed very dramatically afterwards. Pacifists and critics were unpopular but:

> in the end they won. [Cobden](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Cobden "Richard Cobden") and [Bright](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Bright "John Bright") were true to their principles of foreign policy, which laid down the absolute minimum of intervention in European affairs and a deep moral reprobation of war... When the first enthusiasm was passed, when the dead were mourned, the sufferings revealed, and the cost counted, when in 1870 Russia was able calmly to secure the revocation of the Treaty, which disarmed her in the Black Sea, the view became general of the war was stupid and unnecessary, and effected nothing... The Crimean war remained as a classic example... of how governments may plunge into war, how strong ambassadors may mislead weak prime ministers, how the public may be worked up into a facile fury, and how the achievements of the war may crumble to nothing. The Bright-Cobden criticism of the war was remembered and to a large extent accepted \[especially by the Liberal Party\]. Isolation from European entanglements seemed more than ever desirable.<sup id="cite_ref-189" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-189"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>185<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2011467–480_190-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2011467%E2%80%93480-190"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>186<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

As the memory of the "Charge of the Light Brigade" demonstrates, the war became an iconic symbol of logistical, medical and tactical failures and mismanagement. Public opinion in Britain was outraged at the logistical and command failures of the war; the newspapers demanded drastic reforms, and parliamentary investigations demonstrated the multiple failures of the army.<sup id="cite_ref-191" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-191"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>187<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The reform campaign was not well organised, and the traditional aristocratic leadership of the army pulled itself together, and blocked all serious reforms. No one was punished. The outbreak of the [Indian Rebellion of 1857](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Rebellion_of_1857 "Indian Rebellion of 1857") shifted attention to the heroic defence of British interest by the army, and further talk of reform went nowhere.<sup id="cite_ref-192" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-192"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>188<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The demand for professionalisation was achieved by [Florence Nightingale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Florence_Nightingale "Florence Nightingale"), who gained worldwide attention for pioneering and publicising modern nursing while treating the wounded.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2010469–471_193-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2010469%E2%80%93471-193"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>189<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> Another nurse, a Jamaican named [Mary Seacole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mary_Seacole "Mary Seacole"), also made an impact providing care for wounded and dying soldiers. *[The Times](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Times "The Times")* war correspondent [William Howard Russell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Howard_Russell "William Howard Russell") spoke highly of Seacole's skill as a healer, writing "A more tender or skilful hand about a wound or a broken limb could not be found among our best surgeons."<sup id="cite_ref-194" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-194"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>190<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

Outstanding achievements in battlefield surgery were done during the war of 1853–56. "[Nikolai Pirogov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nikolai_Pirogov "Nikolai Pirogov"), who pioneered the system of field surgery that other nations came to only in the First World War".<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEFiges2011295_195-0" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFiges2011295-195"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>191<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

The Crimean War also saw the first tactical use of railways and other modern inventions, such as the electric telegraph, with the first "live" war reporting by Russell. Some credit Russell with prompting the resignation of the sitting British government through his reporting of the lacklustre condition of British forces deployed in Crimea. Additionally, the telegraph reduced the independence of [British overseas possessions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Empire "British Empire") from their commanders in London due to such rapid communications. Newspaper readership informed public opinion in the United Kingdom and France as never before.<sup id="cite_ref-196" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-196"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>192<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>

The Crimean War was a contributing factor in the Russian [abolition of serfdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emancipation_reform_of_1861 "Emancipation reform of 1861") in 1861: Tsar [Alexander II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander_II_of_Russia "Alexander II of Russia") (Nicholas I's son and successor) saw the military defeat of the Russian serf-army by free troops from Britain and France as proof of the need for emancipation.<sup id="cite_ref-197" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-197"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>193<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The Crimean War also led to the realisation by the Russian government of its technological inferiority, in military practices as well as weapons.<sup id="cite_ref-198" class="reference"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/#cite_note-198"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>194<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint noexcerpt Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTRS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOTRS"><span title="The current source is insufficiently reliable. Views of historians are likely reflected, but this is moderated through a third party source (WP:NOTRS). (June 2023)">better&nbsp;source&nbsp;needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Alexander also initiated the [Great Reforms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government_reforms_of_Alexander_II_of_Russia "Government reforms of Alexander II of Russia"), which were aimed at strengthening and modernising the Russian state in the light of weaknesses revealed by the war.
